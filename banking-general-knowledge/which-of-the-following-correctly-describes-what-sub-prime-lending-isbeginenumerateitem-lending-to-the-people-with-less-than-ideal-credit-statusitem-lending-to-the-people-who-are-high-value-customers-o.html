Which of the following correctly describes what sub-prime lending is?

1. :

Lending to the people with less than ideal credit status.

2. :

Lending to the people who are high value customers of the banks.

3. :

Lending to those who are not a regular customer of a bank.

Select the correct answer using the codes given below:

(A) ** Only 1

(B) Only 2

(C) Only 3

(D) All of these

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Subprime lending means giving loans to people who may have difficulty maintaining the repayment schedule, sometimes reflecting setbacks, such as unemployment, medical emergencies, etc.

Concept note-2: -Subprime loans have interest rates that are higher than the prime rate. Subprime borrowers generally have low credit ratings or are people who are perceived of as likely to default on a loan.

Concept note-3: -A subprime mortgage carries an interest rate higher than the rates of prime mortgages. Prime mortgage interest rates are the rates at which banks and other mortgage lenders may lend money to customers with the best credit histories. Prime mortgages can be either fixed or adjustable rate loans.

Concept note-4: -Subprime rates are higher than average interest rates charged on loans to riskier borrowers. These rates are offered, for instance, to borrowers with a poor or thin credit history or low credit score.

Concept note-5: -Those borrows who do not have a good credit history.
