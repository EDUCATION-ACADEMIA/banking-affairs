As per the status on January 28, 2014 the Marginal Standing Facility (MSF) stands at

(A) 8.50%

(B) 8.75%

(C) ** 9.00%

(D) 9.25%

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The current MSF rate or Marginal Standing Facility Rate in India is 4.25%. This is the rate at which the banks can pledge government securities for gaining liquidity in situations when the liquidity is dried up.

Concept note-2: -What is MSF rate India? Currently, the MSF rate of borrowing is 6.25% p.a. which is 25 basis points or 0.25% higher than the Repo rate. Kickstart your UPSC 2023 preparation today!

Concept note-3: -Generally, the MSF rate is 0.25% or 25 basis points more than the repo rate. Using this facility, all the scheduled banks under RBI can avail money in emergency situations up to 1% of their NDTL (net demand and time liabilities) or SLR securities.
