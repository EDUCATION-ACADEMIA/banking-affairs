Agricultural taxation in India is difficult because of

(A) Scattered nature of land holdings

(B) Seasonality of production

(C) The fact that agriculture is not taxed any where in the world

(D) ** Increasing overhead cost of tax collection and the lack of political will

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Agriculture is an informal sector. Almost all the payments are made in cash which makes it difficult to keep a track of income and expenditure. Assessing their true income or income earning potential becomes a burdensome task and if proper revenue is not known, ascertaining the tax amount will get problematic.

Concept note-2: -Agricultural income is not taxable under Section 10 (1) of the Income Tax Act since it is not included in a personâ€™s total income. However, if agricultural revenue surpasses Rs. 5, 000 per year, the state government may levy a tax on it.

Concept note-3: -As per section 10(1), agricultural income earned by the taxpayer in India is exempt from tax. Agricultural income is defined under section 2(1A) of the Income-tax Act.
