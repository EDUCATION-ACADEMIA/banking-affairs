Sending and receiving messages through speaking, listening, and writing is called:

(A) Communication Skills

(B) Non-Verbal Communication

(C) ** Verbal Communication

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Verbal communication includes spoken and written messages as well as the use of sign language. Non-verbal communication happens without the use of a linguistic system. There are many forms of non-verbal communication, for example, using body language, body position, touch, and intonation.

Concept note-2: -In the literal sense, verbal communication is oral communication with words that you or others speak out loud. On the other hand, nonverbal communication is about whatâ€™s not said out loud: gestures, facial expressions, and body language.

Concept note-3: -Verbal communication refers to the use of speech to send a message.

Concept note-4: -Communication is the process of sending and receiving messages through verbal or nonverbal means, including speech, or oral communication; writing and graphical representations (such as infographics, maps, and charts); and signs, signals, and behavior.
