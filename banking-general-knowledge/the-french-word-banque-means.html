The French word Banque means

(A) ** Money Exchange Table

(B) House Exchange Table

(C) Jewellery Exchange Table

(D) None of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The word ‘bank’ is derived from the French word ‘banque’, which means a bench or a money exchange table.

Concept note-2: -Noun. banque f (plural banques) bank (establishment where financial transactions take place)

Concept note-3: -Etymology 1 Borrowed from Italian banco, from Old High German bank, from Proto-Germanic *bankiz.

Concept note-4: -The word bank was borrowed in Middle English from Middle French banque, from Old Italian banca, meaning “table", from Old High German banc, bank “bench, counter".

Concept note-5: -The literal meaning of Italian banca was “bench, ” but the word was also used for the benchlike counter at which an early money changer transacted business, and later to describe the money changer’s shop itself-the bank.
