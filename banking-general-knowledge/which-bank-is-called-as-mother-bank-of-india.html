Which bank is called as “ Mother bank of India “

(A) Canada bank

(B) Corporation bank

(C) State bank of India

(D) ** Reserve Bank of India

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Why is Reserve Bank of India called ‘ Mother of Banks ‘?

Concept note-2: -The Reserve Bank of India is the central bank of the country.

Concept note-3: -Answer: RBI called the “mother of all banks” discussed below: The RBI controls the flow of currency within the economy.

Concept note-4: -The Reserve Bank of India was established on April 1, 1935 in accordance with the provisions of the Reserve Bank of India Act, 1934. The Central Office of the Reserve Bank was initially established in Kolkata but was permanently moved to Mumbai in 1937.

Concept note-5: -In India, Reserve Bank Of India or RBI is known as the banker’s bank. It is so called because it acts as a bank for all the commercial banks in India.
