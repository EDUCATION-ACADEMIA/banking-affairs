(A) Loans from financial institutions

(B) Loans from provident funds and reserve funds

(C) ** Loans from central government

(D) Market borrowings

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -There are two sources to finance the fiscal deficit. They are: Borrowings: internally from a commercial bank, or from external sources like the IMF, other governments, etc. Deficit financing (that is, printing new currency): borrowing funds from RBI against its securities (so, RBI prints new currency).

Concept note-2: -Description: The gross fiscal deficit (GFD) is the excess of total expenditure including loans net of recovery over revenue receipts (including external grants) and non-debt capital receipts. The net fiscal deficit is the gross fiscal deficit less net lending of the Central government.

Concept note-3: -In Union Budget 2023-24, the fiscal deficit to GDP is pegged at 5.9% in FY24. This ratio has declined from 6.4% in 2022-23 (revised estimate) and 6.7% in 2021-22 (actual). In the revenue budget, the deficit was 4.1% of GDP in 2022-23 (revised estimate). In Union Budget 2023-24, revenue deficit is 2.9% of GDP.
