The Tax on import and export is known as____

(A) Income Tax

(B) Trade Tax

(C) ** Custom Duty

(D) Excise Duty

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Customs duty refers to the tax imposed on goods when they are transported across international borders. In simple terms, it is the tax that is levied on import and export of goods. The government uses this duty to raise its revenues, safeguard domestic industries, and regulate movement of goods.

Concept note-2: -tariff, also called customs duty, tax levied upon goods as they cross national boundaries, usually by the government of the importing country.

Concept note-3: -Definition: Customs Duty is a tax imposed on imports and exports of goods. Description: The rates of customs duties are either specific or on ad valorem basis, that is, it is based on the value of goods.

Concept note-4: -Customs duties are charges levied on goods when they cross international borders. Customs duties are charged by special authorities and bodies created by local governments and are meant to protect local industries, economies, and businesses.

Concept note-5: -Import duty is also known as customs duty, tariff, import tax or import tariff. Import duty is levied when imported goods first enter the country.
