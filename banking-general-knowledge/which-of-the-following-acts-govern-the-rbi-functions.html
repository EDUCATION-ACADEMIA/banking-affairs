Which of the following acts govern the RBI functions?

(A) ** RBI Act, 1934

(B) Banking Regulation Act, 1949

(C) Companies Act, 1956

(D) Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1973

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Reserve Bank of India was set up on the basis of the recommendations of the Hilton Young Commission. The Reserve Bank of India Act, 1934 (II of 1934) provides the statutory basis of the functioning of the Bank, which commenced operations on April 1, 1935.

Concept note-2: -The Banking Regulation Act, 1949 empowers the Reserve Bank of India to inspect and supervise commercial banks. The Reserve Bank of India Act, 1934 requires the Central Government to entrust the Reserve Bank with all its money, remittance, exchange and banking transactions in India and the management of its public debt.

Concept note-3: -Under the Reserve Bank of India, Act, 1934 (RBI Act, 1934) (as amended in 2016), RBI is entrusted with the responsibility of conducting monetary policy in India with the primary objective of maintaining price stability while keeping in mind the objective of growth.
