In India, the first bank of limited liabilities managed by Indians and founded in 1881 was

(A) Hindustan Commercial Bank

(B) ** Oudh Commercial Bank

(C) Punjab National Bank

(D) Punjab and Sind Bank

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Muskan Anand. Oudh Commercial Bank or Awadh Commercial Bank was an Indian bank established in 1881 in Faizabad and operated until 1958 when it failed. It was the first commercial bank in India having limited liability and an entirely Indian board of directors.

Concept note-2: -Detailed Solution Oudh Commercial Bank was established in 1881 in Faizabad. It was the first commercial bank of limited liability. This bank was entirely managed by Indian Board of Directors.

Concept note-3: -The first bank purely managed bu Indian was Punjab National Bank, established in Lahore in 1895.

Concept note-4: -The first bank of a joint stock variety was Bank of Bombay, established in 1720 in Bombay4 .

Concept note-5: -The first entirely Indian joint stock bank was the Oudh Commercial Bank, established in 1881 in Faizabad.
