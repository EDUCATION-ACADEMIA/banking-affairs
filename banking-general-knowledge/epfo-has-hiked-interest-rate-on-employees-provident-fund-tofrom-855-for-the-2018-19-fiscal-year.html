EPFO has hiked interest rate on employees’ provident fund to____from 8.55% for the 2018-19 fiscal year.

(A) 8.80%

(B) 8.70%

(C) 8.75%

(D) ** 8.65%

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Revised EPF Interest Rate for 2019-20 : Lowered by 0.15% Earlier in the year 2016-17 and 2018-19, the EPFO had given an 8.65% rate of interest to the subscribers.

Concept note-2: -“The CBT, EPF had recommended 8.10 per cent rate of interest on EPF for the financial year 2021-22, which has been approved by the Government and is higher than other comparable schemes viz. General Provident Fund (7.10%)/Senior Citizens Savings Scheme (7.40%)/Sukanya Samriddhi Account Scheme (7.60%).

Concept note-3: -The EPF Interest Rate for 2022-23 is fixed at 8.10%. This rate is valid for all deposits made from 1st April 2022 to 31st March 2023.

Concept note-4: -Since then, the Employees’ Provident Fund Organisation (EPFO) has split EPF passbooks for 2021-22 into taxable and non-taxable contributions, but the interest credit is still to be made to many EPF account holders.
