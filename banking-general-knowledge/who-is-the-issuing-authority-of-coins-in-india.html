Who is the issuing authority of coins in India?

(A) Reserve Bank of India

(B) ** Government of India

(C) Ministry of Finance

(D) Both 1 and 2

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Reserve Bank is the agent of the Government for distribution, issue and handling of coins. Four mints are in operation: Mumbai in Maharashtra, Noida in Uttar Pradesh, Kolkata, and Hyderabad.

Concept note-2: -The Reserve Bank of India issues notes and coins on behalf of the state banks. Q. The Reserve Bank of India issues currency notes and coins in India.

Concept note-3: -The coins are issued for circulation only through the Reserve Bank in terms of Section 38 of the RBI Act.
