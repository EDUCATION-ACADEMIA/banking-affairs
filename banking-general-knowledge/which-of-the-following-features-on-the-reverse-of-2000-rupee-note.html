Which of the following features on the reverse of 2000 rupee note?

(A) ** Mangalyaan

(B) Himalaya Mountains

(C) Red Fort

(D) Parliament of India

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The reverse side of 2000 rupee note features Mangalyaan, India’s first interplanetary space mission.

Concept note-2: -The new denomination has motif of the Mangalyaan on the reverse, depicting the country’s first venture in interplanetary space. The base colour of the note is magenta.

Concept note-3: -The new denomination has motif of the Mangalyaan on the reverse. The base colour of the note is magenta. The note has other designs, geometric patterns aligning with the overall colour scheme, both on the obverse and the reverse. The size of the new note is 66mm x 166mm.

Concept note-4: -It is one of the oldest stone structures in India. In the year 1989, Sanchi Stupa was declared as World Heritage Site by UNESCO. For all these reasons, we see Sanchi Stupa as the motif on Indian currency.
