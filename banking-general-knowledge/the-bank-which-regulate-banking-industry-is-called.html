The bank which regulate banking industry is called

(A) State Bank

(B) ** Central Bank

(C) Icici Bank

(D) HdFC bank

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A central bank, reserve bank, or monetary authority is an institution that manages the currency and monetary policy of a country or monetary union, and oversees their commercial banking system. In contrast to a commercial bank, a central bank possesses a monopoly on increasing the monetary base.

Concept note-2: -The Reserve Bank of India is the central bank of the country.

Concept note-3: -A central bank is a public institution that is responsible for implementing monetary policy, managing the currency of a country, or group of countries, and controlling the money supply.

Concept note-4: -A central bank also acts as the regulatory authority of a country’s monetary policy and is the sole provider and printer of notes and coins in circulation.

Concept note-5: -Thus, Reserve Bank acts as a common banker, known as ‘Banker to banks’ function, the operational instructions for which are issued by concerned central office departments of the Reserve Bank. Among other provisions, the Reserve Bank stipulates minimum balances to be maintained by banks in these accounts.
