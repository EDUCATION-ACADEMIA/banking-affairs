Which is not an example of near money?

(A) Bill of exchange

(B) Govt. bonds and debentures

(C) ** Equity shares of Ranbaxy company limited

(D) Treasury bills of the Government of India

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A few examples of near money are treasury bills, bank deposits, bonds, and money markets, etc. Cash like paper notes and coins are not near money.

Concept note-2: -Examples of near money assets include savings accounts, certificates of deposit (CDs), foreign currencies, money market accounts, marketable securities, and Treasury bills (T-bills).

Concept note-3: -’Near Money’ refers to non-cash assets which are highly liquid, such as bank deposits (saving deposits, current deposits etc), treasury bills, money markets, widely traded foreign currencies etc.

Concept note-4: -The cheque is also called “near money”.
