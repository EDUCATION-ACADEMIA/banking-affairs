As per Section 23 (1) of Banking Regulation Act 1949, a banking company is exempted from obtaining prior permission from RBI for:

(A) ** Change of premises within the same city, town or village

(B) Opening of an extension counter

(C) Providing temporary banking facilities in any institution

(D) Cash Reserve Ratio

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The mandate and related documentation which forms the basis for effecting payments for such transactions carried out over the ATMs should be settled bilaterally between the bank and customers and the rights and obligations of each party should be clearly stated in the mandate and should be valid in the court of law.

Concept note-2: -Primary Agricultural Credit Society and cooperative land mortgage banks are excluded from the Act.

Concept note-3: -Section 20 of Banking Regulation Act, 1949 (B.R. Act, 1949) prohibits banks from granting any loan or advance to any of its Directors.

Concept note-4: -Trading is prohibited under Section 8 of this Act. No banking company shall directly or indirectly deal in the buying or selling, or bartering of goods except when it is selling the goods kept in its security.
