Assets-Liabilities =

(A) ** Net worth

(B) Debts

(C) Budget

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Net worth is assets minus liabilities. Or, you can think of net worth as everything you own less all that you owe.

Concept note-2: -To calculate your net worth, you subtract your total liabilities from your total assets. Total assets will include your investments, savings, cash deposits, and any equity that you have in a home, car, or other similar assets. Total liabilities would include any debt, such as student loans and credit card debt.

Concept note-3: -Net worth is the total value of all assets minus any liabilities. Put simply, net worth is what you own minus what you owe. Calculating net worth can be a helpful way to determine one’s wealth and the overall health of a person’s or company’s financial situation.

Concept note-4: -Start with what you own: cash, retirement accounts, investment accounts, cars, real estate and anything else that you could sell for cash. Then subtract what you owe: credit card debt, student loans, mortgages, auto loans and anything else you owe money on. Then boom-you’ve got your net worth.

Concept note-5: -Your net worth is the amount by which your assets exceed your liabilities. In simple terms, net worth is the difference between what you own and what you owe. If your assets exceed your liabilities, you have a positive net worth.
