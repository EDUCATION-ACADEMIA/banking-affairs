In 3rd Bi-monthly Policy Statement, RBI’s monetary policy committee (MPC) raised policy rates by 25 basis points to 6.5% on account of inflationary pressures arising due to hike in minimum support price. What is the Bank rate at present (As on 02nd August 2018)?

(A) 6.25%

(B) 6.75%

(C) 6.55%

(D) ** 6.00%

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Consequently, the standing deposit facility (SDF) rate stands adjusted to 6.25 per cent and the marginal standing facility (MSF) rate and the Bank Rate to 6.75 per cent.

Concept note-2: -Highlights of RBI’s Bi-monthly Monetary Policy of 2022-23; Repo Rate raised to 6.25% in December 2022.

Concept note-3: -Reserve Bank of India governor Shaktikanta Das, in the briefing after the monetary policy meeting, said that the real gross domestic product (GDP) growth for the financial year 2023 is estimated to be at 6.8 percent.

Concept note-4: -Mumbai, 7 December 2022 Consequently, the standing deposit facility (SDF) rate stands adjusted to 6.00 per cent, and the marginal standing facility (MSF) rate and the Bank Rate to 6.50 per cent.
