What two items do you need to reconcile your checking account?

(A) ** Check register and monthly bank statement

(B) Credit card statement and monthly bank statement

(C) Check register and credit card statement

(D) Check register and voided checks

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -To do a bank reconciliation you would match the cash balances on the balance sheet to the corresponding amount on your bank statement, determining the differences between the two in order to make changes to the accounting records, resolve any discrepancies and identify fraudulent transactions.

Concept note-2: -A bank reconciliation helps ensure that your ending bank statement and your general ledger account are in balance. Remember, your cash or bank accounts in your general ledger should reflect the same activity that is on your bank statement. If it doesn’t, you need to determine what’s missing.

Concept note-3: -A bank reconciliation statement is a document prepared by a company that shows its recorded bank account balance matches the balance the bank lists. This statement includes all transactions, such as deposits and withdrawals, from a given timeframe.
