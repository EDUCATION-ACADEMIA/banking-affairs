When did the Banking Regulation Act, 1949 become applicable to the state of Jammu & Kashmir?

(A) 1949

(B) 1970

(C) ** 1956

(D) 1966

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Passed as the Banking Companies Act 1949, it came into force from 16 March 1949 and changed to Banking Regulation Act 1949 from 1 March 1966. It is applicable in Jammu and Kashmir from 1956. Initially, the law was applicable only to banking companies.

Concept note-2: -[10th March, 1949] An Act to consolidate and amend the law relating to banking 2[***].

Concept note-3: -An Overview: Banking Regulation Act, 1949 Passed as the Banking Companies Act 1949, it came into power from Sixteen March 1949 and changed to Banking Regulation Act 1949 from first March 1966.

Concept note-4: –(1) No co-operative society other than a co-operative bank shall use as part of its name or in connection with its business any of the words “bank”, “banker” or “banking”, and no co-operative society shall carry on the business of banking in India unless it uses as part of its name at least of such words.

Concept note-5: -(1) This Act may be called the Banking 1 [Regulation] Act, 1949.
