Which of the following statements are true about Working Capital?

1. :

Working Capital is a financial metric which represents operating liquidity available to a business, organization, or other entity, including government entity.

2. :

Net working capital is calculated as current assets minus current liabilities

3. :

The management of Working Capital involves managing inventories, accounts receivable and payable, and cash.

(A) Only 1

(B) Only 2

(C) Only 3

(D) ** All are true

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Working capital is a financial metric that is the difference between a companyâ€™s curent assets and current liabilities. As a financial metric, working capital helps plan for future needs and ensure the company has enough cash and cash equivalents meet short-term obligations, such as unpaid taxes and short-term debt.

Concept note-2: -Working capital can be defined as the difference between the current assets and liabilities.

Concept note-3: -Net working capital is defined as current assets minus the difference between current liabilities and notes payable, and any decrease in the current ratio automatically indicates that net working capital has decreased.

Concept note-4: -The elements of working capital are money coming in, money going out, and the management of inventory. Companies must also prepare reliable cash forecasts and maintain accurate data on transactions and bank balances.
