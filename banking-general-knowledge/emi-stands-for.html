EMI stands for

(A) Equal Monthly plan

(B) ** Equated Monthly Instalments

(C) Equal payment option

(D) Equated money installments

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -An equated monthly installment (EMI) is a fixed payment amount made by a borrower to a lender at a specified date each calendar month. Equated monthly installments are applied to both interest and principal each month so that over a specified number of years, the loan is paid off in full.

Concept note-2: -What does EMI mean? EMI stands for equated monthly instalment. It relates to payments made regularly to repay an outstanding loan within a certain time frame. As the name implies, these instalments are always of the same amount.

Concept note-3: -Because every monthly payment is equal, these payments are called “equated monthly installments.” You agree to make those payments until the loan is paid in full. An equated monthly installment (EMI) includes principal, interest, and sometimes, fees rolled into the loan by the lender.

Concept note-4: -EMI Calculation Methods Calculating EMI has a Simple Formula, Which is As Follows: EMI = (P X R/12) X [(1+R/12) N] / [(1+R/12) N-1]. Here, P is the original loan amount or principal, R is the rate of interest that is applicable per annum and N is the number of monthly installments/ loan tenure.
