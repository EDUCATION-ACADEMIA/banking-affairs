Merchant banking in India is governed by

(A) ** SEBI

(B) IRDA

(C) RBI

(D) Ministry of Finance

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The SEBI or Securities and Exchange Board of India is the regulatory authority for Merchant Banking in India. The law which regulates the activities of the Merchant Bank in India is the SEBI (Merchant Bankers) Regulations 1992. For setting up a Merchant Bank, the concerned individual has to approach the SEBI.

Concept note-2: -Yes. As per regulation 9A of SEBI (Merchant Bankers) Regulations, 1992, change in control shall affect the obligation to obtain a fresh registration under section 12 of the SEBI Act, 1992. Further, a Merchant Banker is required to obtain fresh certificate of registration within a period of six months.
