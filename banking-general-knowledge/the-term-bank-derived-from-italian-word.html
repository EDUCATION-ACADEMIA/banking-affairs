The term bank derived from Italian word

(A) Bank

(B) ** Banco

(C) Banque

(D) Banko

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The word bank is derived from the Italian word Banco or from a French word Banque, which means a bench or money exchange table.

Concept note-2: -Etymology. Adjective. Italian, money of account, bank, bench, variant of banca bank, bench.

Concept note-3: -Etymology 1 Borrowed from Italian banco, from Old High German bank, from Proto-Germanic *bankiz.

Concept note-4: -banque f (plural banques) bank (establishment where financial transactions take place)

Concept note-5: -There are others, who are of the opinion that the word “bank” is originally derived from the German word “back” meaning a joint stock fund, which was Italianized into “banco” when the Germans were masters of a great part of Italy.
