At which rate the RBI lends short-term money to the bank?

(A) PLR

(B) CRR

(C) ** Repo rate

(D) Reverse repo rate

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -What is the Current Repo Rate? Repo Rate (RR) is the rate at which the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) lends money to commercial banks or financial institutions in India against government securities. The current Repo Rate 2022 is at 4.40%.

Concept note-2: -Comparatively, Bank Rate caters to long term financial requirements of commercial banks whereas Repo Rate focuses on short term financial needs.

Concept note-3: -Reserve Bank of India (RBI), the central banking institution of India controls the monetary policy of the Indian currency. The key repo rate has been hiked on 7 December 2022 by the Monetary Policy Committee (MPC) of the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) by 35 basis point to 6.25%.

Concept note-4: -Definition: Repo rate is the rate at which the central bank of a country (Reserve Bank of India in case of India) lends money to commercial banks in the event of any shortfall of funds. Repo rate is used by monetary authorities to control inflation.

Concept note-5: -Repo Rate meaning: Repo Rate, or repurchase rate, is the key monetary policy rate of interest at which the central bank or the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) lends short term money to banks, essentially to control credit availability, inflation, and the economic growth.
