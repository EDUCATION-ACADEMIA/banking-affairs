National Income indicates

(A) The number of families below poverty line.

(B) Goods and Services in Economy

(C) Growth of Economy

(D) ** B and C

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The formula of National Income is: NI = C (household consumption) + G (government expenditure) + I (investment expense) + NX (net exports).

Concept note-2: -The broadest and most widely used measure of national income is gross domestic product (GDP), the value of expenditures on final goods and services at market prices produced by domestic factors of production (labor, capital, materials) during the year.

Concept note-3: -National Income is defined as the total monetary value of all goods and services produced within a country during a given period of time. It is an important indicator of the economic activities that are taking place within a nation.
