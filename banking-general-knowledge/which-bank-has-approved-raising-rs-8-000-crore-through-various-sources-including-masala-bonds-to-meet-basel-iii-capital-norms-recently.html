Which Bank has approved raising Rs 8, 000 crore through various sources, including masala bonds, to meet Basel III capital norms recently?

(A) ** State Bank of India

(B) ICICI Bank

(C) Punjab National Bank

(D) HDFC Bank

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In India, currently Bandhan Bank has the highest capital adequacy ratio.

Concept note-2: -Basel Norm III is also known as the Third Basel Accord or Basel Standards. It is a regulatory framework followed on a voluntary basis on a global scale. The framework deals with capital adequacy in banks, stress testing, and market liquidity risk.

Concept note-3: -RBI voluntarily adopted capital adequacy regulations in April 1992 for the Indian banking sector, thus complying with the best practices of Basel I norms recommended by BCBS in 1988. These capital adequacy norms were revised to Basel II and subsequently upgraded to Basel III.

Concept note-4: -Total regulatory capital should be at least 9% of risk weighted assets and within this, Tier 1 capital should be at least 6% of risk weighted assets. Within Tier 1 capital, innovative Tier 1 instruments are limited to 15% of Tier 1 capital.
