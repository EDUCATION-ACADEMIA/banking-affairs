The Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) is the regulator for the securities market in India. It was established on

(A) ** 12th April 1992

(B) 12th April 1988

(C) 12th April 1990

(D) 12th April 1982

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Establishment Of SEBI The Securities and Exchange Board of India was established on April 12, 1992 in accordance with the provisions of the Securities and Exchange Board of India Act, 1992.

Concept note-2: -Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) was first established in 1988 as a non-statutory body for regulating the securities market. It became an autonomous body on 30 January 1992 and was accorded statutory powers with the passing of the SEBI Act 1992 by the Indian Parliament.

Concept note-3: -An Act to provide for the establishment of a Board to protect the interests of investors in securities and to promote the development of, and to regulate, the securities market and for matters connected therewith or incidental thereto.
