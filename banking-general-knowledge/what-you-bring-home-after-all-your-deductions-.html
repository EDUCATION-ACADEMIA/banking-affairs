What you bring home after all your Deductions? *

(A) Gross Pay

(B) Savings

(C) Annual Pay

(D) ** Net Pay

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Net salary, also known as take-home salary, is the amount of money that you will receive after all deductions. The deductions are made from the CTC and include things like income tax, Professional tax, Public Provident Fund (PPF), etc. Net salary is usually lower than the gross salary.

Concept note-2: -Gross pay is what employees earn before taxes, benefits and other payroll deductions are withheld from their wages. The amount remaining after all withholdings are accounted for is net pay or take-home pay.

Concept note-3: -Net income (NI) is calculated as revenues minus expenses, interest, and taxes.

Concept note-4: -The formula to calculate net salary is quite simple. Net Salary = Gross Salary-Deductions.
