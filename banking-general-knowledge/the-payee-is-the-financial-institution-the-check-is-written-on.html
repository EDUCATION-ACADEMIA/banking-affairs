The payee is the financial institution the check is written on.

(A) True

(B) ** False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -You need to draw two parallel crossed lines at the top left side of the instrument. You will then need to write ‘A/C Payee Only’. You can also write “Account payee” on the cheque leaf. If you don’t write “Account payee”, then it will be processed as a crossed cheque instead.

Concept note-2: -On the line that says “Pay to the order of, ” write the name of the individual or company you’d like to pay, known as the payee. Use the payee’s full name instead of a nickname.

Concept note-3: -A payee is the term used to describe the person or business that you are paying money to.

Concept note-4: -Endorse: To sign your name on the back of a check in order to cash it or deposit it. Financial Institution: A business that deals with money. For example, a bank or credit union.
