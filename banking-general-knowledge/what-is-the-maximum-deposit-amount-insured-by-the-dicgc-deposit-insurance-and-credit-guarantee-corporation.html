What is the maximum deposit amount insured by the DICGC (Deposit Insurance and Credit Guarantee Corporation)?

(A) ** Rupees One Lakh

(B) Rupees Two Lakh

(C) Rupees Three Lakh

(D) Rupees Five Lakh

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The DICGC insures principal and interest upto a maximum amount of five lakhs.

Concept note-2: -The DICGC insures principal and interest upto a maximum amount of ₹ five lakhs. For example, if an individual had an account with a principal amount of 4, 95, 000 plus accrued interest of 4, 000, the total amount insured by the DICGC would be 4, 99, 000.

Concept note-3: -If a bank goes into liquidation, DICGC is liable to pay to the liquidator the claim amount of each depositor upto Rupees five lakhs within two months from the date of receipt of claim list from the liquidator. The liquidator has to disburse the claim amount to each insured depositor corresponding to their claim amount.

Concept note-4: -The DICGC insures all deposits such as Savings, Fixed, Current, Recurring etc. Each depositor in a bank is insured up to a maximum of ₹ 5, 00, 000 (Rupees Five Lakhs) for both principal and interest amount held by him/her in the same right and capacity.

Concept note-5: -Which banks are insured by the DICGC? Commercial Banks : All commercial banks including branches of foreign banks functioning in India, local area banks and regional rural banks are insured by the DICGC.
