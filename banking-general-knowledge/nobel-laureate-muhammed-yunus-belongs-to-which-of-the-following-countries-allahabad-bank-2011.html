Nobel laureate Muhammed Yunus belongs to which of the following countries? [Allahabad Bank 2011]

(A) Sri Lanka

(B) Pakistan

(C) Maldives

(D) ** Bangladesh

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Muhammad Yunus and Grameen Bank were awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for 2006 for their work to “create economic and social development from below”.

Concept note-2: -Photography: Jared Leeds Muhammad Yunus won a Nobel Peace Prize for spreading the concept of microcredit-tiny loans to help poor people start businesses-via his Bangladesh-based Grameen Bank.

Concept note-3: -The Norwegian Nobel Committee has decided to award the Nobel Peace Prize for 2006, divided into two equal parts, to Muhammad Yunus and Grameen Bank for their efforts to create economic and social development from below.

Concept note-4: -Banker to the Poorest of the Poor When Bangladesh suffered a famine in 1974, he felt that he had to do something more for the poor beyond simply teaching. He decided to give long-term loans to people who wanted to start their own small enterprises. This initiative was extended on a larger scale through Grameen Bank.
