Which of the following is also known as Prepaid Debit cards?

(A) Loadable Debit Cards

(B) ** Reloadable Debit Cards

(C) Relational Debit Cards

(D) None of The Above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A prepaid card is also called a prepaid debit card, or a stored-value card. You can buy prepaid cards at many stores and online. Many prepaid cards come with the Visa or MasterCard logo.

Concept note-2: -A reloadable, or prepaid, debit card is not the same as a debit card thatâ€™s linked to your bank account. Reloadable debit cards allow you to add money to them as needed. Some reloadable debit cards charge a fee for adding more money to your balance. They may have other fees, too.

Concept note-3: -A reloadable prepaid card allows you to add more money. This type of card is sometimes called a General Purpose Reloadable Card, or GPR Card. Some cards start out as non-reloadable, but can be reloaded once you complete a registration process.

Concept note-4: -Definition and Example of a Prepaid Debit Card Some prepaid debit cards come from major companies like Visa or Mastercard and offer rewards like a credit card. Others are for specific purposes, like a college student who has a prepaid card used to access student services and cafeterias.

Concept note-5: -Debit cards are payment cards that are connected to a bank account, from which they withdraw funds as you make purchases. Prepaid cards, on the other hand, are not connected to a bank account. Instead, you load money directly onto the card.
