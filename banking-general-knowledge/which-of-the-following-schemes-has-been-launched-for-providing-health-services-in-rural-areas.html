Which of the following schemes has been launched for providing health services in rural areas?

(A) Operation Flood

(B) Look East

(C) SJSRY

(D) ** ASHA

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The National Rural Health Mission (NRHM) was launched by the Honâ€™ble Prime Minister to provide accessible, affordable and quality health care to the rural population, especially the vulnerable groups.

Concept note-2: -One of the key strategies under the National Rural Health Mission (NHM) is having a Community Health Volunteer i.e. ASHA (Accredited Social Health Activist) for every village with a population of 1000. Detailed guidelines have been issued by the Government of India in matter of selection and training of ASHA.

Concept note-3: -ASHA will mobilise the community and facilitate them in accessing health and health related services available at the Anganwadi/sub-centre/primary health centers, such as immunisation, Ante Natal Check-up (ANC), Post Natal Check-up supplementary nutrition, sanitation and other services being provided by the government.

Concept note-4: -India launched the ASHA programme in 2005-06 as part of the National Rural Health Mission.

Concept note-5: -ASHA will take steps to create awareness and provide information to the community on determinants of health such as nutrition, basic sanitation & hygienic practices, healthy living and working conditions, information on existing health services and the need for timely utilization of health & family welfare services.
