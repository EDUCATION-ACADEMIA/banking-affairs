Which among the following schemes of the Government was launched with an objective of a slum free India?

1. :

Indira Awas Yojana

2. :

Rajiv Awas Yojana

3. :

Ambedkar Awas Yojana

4. :

Grahalaxmi Scheme

(A) Only 1

(B) ** Only 2

(C) Only 3

(D) Only 4

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Detailed Solution. The Correct answer is Valmiki Ambedkar Awaas Yojana. It was an central government scheme which focused on the upliftment of living condition of people in urban slums across the country. It was launched on December 2, 2001.

Concept note-2: -Rajiv Awas Yojana (RAY) envisages a “Slum Free India” with inclusive and equitable cities in which every citizen has access to basic civic infrastructure, social amenities and decent shelter. Encourage States/Union Territories (UTs) to tackle slums in a definitive manner, by focusing on: 2.1.

Concept note-3: -Keeping this in mind, the Rajiv Gandhi Awas Yojana housing scheme was launched in 2011 by the Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs (MoHUA) in collaboration with several state governments, including Karnataka, Rajasthan and Gujarat.
