What is the time period for a term loan to be classified as NPA?

(A) 30 days

(B) 60 days

(C) ** 90 days

(D) 365 days

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -If the interest or principal remains overdue for a period 90 days or three months and above the loan account is classified as a Non-Performing Asset (NPA).

Concept note-2: -The banking sector follows an automated system for tagging accounts as NPAs, under which the accounts are tagged as NPAs on the day the account becomes overdue for more than 90 days. However, in many NBFCs, this classification is made after the end of 90 or 180 days.

Concept note-3: -For the above category of banks, an account would be classified as Non Performing Asset if the : (i) Interest and/or installment of principal remain overdue for a period of more than 180 days in respect of a Term Loan.

Concept note-5: -2004 the norm for classification of an asset as non performing has been reduced to 90 days from 180 days.
