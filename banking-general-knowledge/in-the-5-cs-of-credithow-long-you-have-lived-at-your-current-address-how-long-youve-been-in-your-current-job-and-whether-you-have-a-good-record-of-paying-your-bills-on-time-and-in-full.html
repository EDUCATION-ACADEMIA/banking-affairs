(A) ** Character

(B) Capacity

(C) Capital

(D) Collateral

(E) Conditions

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The lender will typically follow what is called the Five Cs of Credit: Character, Capacity, Capital, Collateral and Conditions. Examining each of these things helps the lender determine the level of risk associated with providing the borrower with the requested funds.

Concept note-2: -Capacity Capacity measures the borrower’s ability to repay a loan by comparing income against recurring debts and assessing the borrower’s debt-to-income (DTI) ratio. Lenders calculate DTI by adding a borrower’s total monthly debt payments and dividing that by the borrower’s gross monthly income.

Concept note-3: -This system is called the 5 Cs of credit-Character, Capacity, Capital, Conditions, and Collateral.

Concept note-4: -1. Character. A lender will look at a mortgage applicant’s overall trustworthiness, personality and credibility to determine the borrower’s character. The purpose of this is to determine whether the applicant is responsible and likely to make on-time payments on loans and other debts.
