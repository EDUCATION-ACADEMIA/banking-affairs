Under the guidelines issued on 2 Dec 2011, an NBFC-MFI should have minimum net owned funds of how much amount?

(A) Rs. 3 crore

(B) Rs. 4 crore

(C) ** Rs. 5 crore

(D) Rs. 6 crore

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In terms of Section 45-IA of the RBI Act, 1934, no Non-banking Financial company can commence or carry on business of a non-banking financial institution without a) obtaining a certificate of registration from the Bank and without having a Net Owned Funds of ₹ 25 lakhs (₹ Two crore since April 1999).

Concept note-2: -Every company seeking registration as NBFC-Factor shall have a minimum Net Owned Fund (NOF) of ₹5 crore, or as specified by the Reserve Bank from time to time.

Concept note-3: -20 crore or more (irrespective of their asset size) as per their audited balance sheet as of 31 March 2001 would be required to put in place the ALM System.
