Commercial bank create money by way of:

(A) Time deposits

(B) ** Demand deposits

(C) Treasury bills

(D) Bill of exchange

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Commercial banks make money by providing and earning interest from loans such as mortgages, auto loans, business loans, and personal loans. Customer deposits provide banks with the capital to make these loans.

Concept note-2: -Using demand deposits, commercial banks create money.

Concept note-3: -Answer: 1. Derivative deposits also known as secondary deposits are the deposits in excess of the minimum cash reserves to be held by the banks. These deposits are used to grant loans to the borrowers. In this way, derivative deposits form the basis of credit creation by the commercial banks.

Concept note-4: -“When banks extend loans to their customers, they create money by crediting their customers’ accounts.”
