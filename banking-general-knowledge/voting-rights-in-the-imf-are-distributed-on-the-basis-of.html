Voting rights in the IMF are distributed on the basis of

(A) one country, one vote

(B) proportion to the share of the income of the country in the world income

(C) proportion to contributions given by each country

(D) ** proportion to quota allotted to countries from time to time

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Voting power in the IMF is purely based on a quota system. So, voting rights in the IMF are distributed on the basis of proportion to quota allotted to countries from time to time.

Concept note-2: -Unlike the United Nations General Assembly, where each country has one vote, voting power and decision-making at the IMF reflect its member countries’ relative economic position.

Concept note-3: -Quotas are the IMF’s main source of financing. Each member of the IMF is assigned a quota, based broadly on its relative position in the world economy. The IMF regularly reviews quotas to assess their adequacy overall and their distribution among members.

Concept note-4: -India’s quota in the IMF, which determines voting shares in the multi-lateral lending agency, is 2.75 per cent.

Concept note-5: -7. Which of the following statement is NOT correct about the quota at the IMF? Explanation: China has third highest quota in the IMF.
