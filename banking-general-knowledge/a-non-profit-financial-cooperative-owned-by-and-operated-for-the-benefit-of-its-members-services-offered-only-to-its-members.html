a non profit financial cooperative owned by and operated for the benefit of its members; services offered only to its members

(A) ** credit union

(B) commercial bank

(C) savings and loans associations

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Yes, credit unions are non-profit organizations owned and controlled by the Members who use their services. Credit unions operate to promote the well-being of their Members. Profits made by credit unions are returned back to Members in the form of reduced fees, higher savings rates, and lower loan rates.

Concept note-2: -A financial cooperative (co-op) is a member-owned, nonprofit financial institution that operates under a members-first philosophy to serve a populationâ€™s banking needs. A financial co-op may be able to offer lower fees and better rates because they are not seeking profits to return to investors.

Concept note-3: -Credit unions are not-for-profit, member-owned, and member-controlled cooperatives organized to provide financial services to their members.

Concept note-4: -Credit unions are not-for-profit organisations owned by their customers, who are also its members. As a member and owner, you are given the opportunity to gain greater control over your finances.
