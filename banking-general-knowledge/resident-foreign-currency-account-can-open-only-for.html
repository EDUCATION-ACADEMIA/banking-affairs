Resident foreign currency account can open only for____

(A) ** Scheduled commercial banks

(B) Public sector banks

(C) Private sector Banks

(D) Regional Rural banks

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A Resident Foreign Currency account in India can be maintained by a Non-resident Indian who has returned home for permanent settlement, after staying abroad for a minimum period of one year. An RFC account can be opened without any regulatory approval from the Reserve Bank of India.

Concept note-2: -Only individual Indian residents are permitted to remit funds under LRS. Corporates, partnership firms, HUF, trusts, etc are excluded from its ambit.
