Special damages refers to damages payable by a banker to his customer for the actual ____ loss suffered by customer

(A) ** Financial

(B) special

(C) Unpecuniary

(D) Unfinancial

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Special damages refer to damages payable by a banker to his customer for the actual financial loss suffered by the customer as a direct result of the wrongful dishonour of his cheque by the banker.

Concept note-2: -money paid to an injured party in a legal case for financial losses such as loss of earnings, property damage and medical expenses.

Concept note-3: -In order to cover a loan in default, a bank has a legal right to seize funds of a guarantor or the debtor. A settlement of mutual debt between a creditor and a debtor through offsetting transaction claims is also known as setoff.
