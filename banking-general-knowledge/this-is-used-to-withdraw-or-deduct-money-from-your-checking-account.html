This is used to withdraw or deduct money from your checking account.

(A) A. credit card

(B) B. debit card

(C) C. ATM card

(D) ** Both B and C

(E) Both A and B

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -When your bank account is debited, money is taken out of the account. The opposite of a debit is a credit, in which case money is added to your account. Your account is debited in many instances.

Concept note-2: -A debit card allows you to withdraw money from their Automated Teller Machines (ATM) any time of the night or day, all through the year. As a result, you can avoid making a trip to a bank branch during banking hours. To withdraw money from an ATM, you just need to insert your debit card and type in a unique password.

Concept note-3: -A cash withdrawal refers to taking money out of a bank account, usually a checking account, in the form of cash.
