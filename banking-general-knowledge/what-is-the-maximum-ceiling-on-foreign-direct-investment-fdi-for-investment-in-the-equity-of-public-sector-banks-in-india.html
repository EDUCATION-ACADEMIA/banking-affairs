What is the maximum ceiling on Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) for investment in the equity of Public Sector Banks in India?

(A) ** 20%

(B) 26%

(C) 49%

(D) 51%

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The government has permitted 20% of Foreign Direct Investment in Banking-public Sector. Foreign investment involves investments of funds abroad in exchange for financial return.

Concept note-2: -FDI is restricted to 49% of the paid up capital of the ARC. However, FIIs registered with SEBI can invest in the Security Receipts (SRs) issued by ARCs registered with RBI.

Concept note-3: -(a) FDI limit in Private Sector Banks is raised to 74 per cent under the automatic route including investment by FIls.
