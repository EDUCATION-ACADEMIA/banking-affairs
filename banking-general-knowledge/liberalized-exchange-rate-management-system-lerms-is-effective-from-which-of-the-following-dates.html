Liberalized Exchange Rate Management System (LERMS) is effective from which of the following dates?

(A) June 1, 1993

(B) ** March1, 1993

(C) April1, 1993

(D) July 1, 1993

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Following the recommendations of the C. Rangarajan Committee, the Liberalized Exchange Rate Management System (LERMS) with the dual exchange rate was implemented. It came into effect on March 1, 1993.

Concept note-2: -Rangarajan) the Liberalised Exchange Rate Management System(LERMS) involving dual exchange rate mechanism was instituted in March 1992 which was followed by the ultimate convergence of the dual rates effective from March 1, 1993(christened modified LERMS).

Concept note-3: -The Indian government introduced the Liberalised Exchange Rate Management System (LERMS) in March 1992. The new system was announced by Manmohan Singh in the 1992 budget and, in early March, by the Reserve Bank of India (RBI). This was the initial step to enable a transition to a market-determined exchange rate system.

Concept note-4: -Since 1973, the floating exchange rate regime was adopted by countries with following options: managed float system, free float system, crawling peg system, currency board arrangement and target zone arrangement.

Concept note-5: -With the abolition of liberalized exchange rate management system (LERMS) in 1993, the exchange rate of the rupee became market determined.
