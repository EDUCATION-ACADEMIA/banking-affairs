The RBI is not expected to perform the function of

(A) the banker to the government

(B) accepting deposit from commercial banks

(C) ** accepting deposit from commercial public

(D) issue of currency

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Which entities can legally accept deposits from public? Banks, including co-operative banks, can accept deposits. Non-bank finance companies, which have been issued Certificate of Registration by RBI with a specific licence to accept deposits, are entitled to accept public deposit.

Concept note-2: -Issuing of coins is not a function of the Central Bank.

Concept note-3: -Hence, Issuing of Notes is not the function of a Commercial Bank. NOTE-The coins are issued for circulation only through the Reserve Bank in terms of the RBI Act.

Concept note-4: -business of a non-banking financial institution are not allowed to accept deposits except from relatives specified and in the manner prescribed in the provision of Section 45-S of the RBI Act.
