When the RBI wants to inject liquidity into economy, it may adopt the following:

1. :

Buy the government securities from the banks.

2. :

Enter into reverse repo operations

3. :

Raise cash Reserve Ratio

4. :

Reduce SLR.

Select the correct answer using the codes given below:

(A) 1 only

(B) ** 1 and 4 only

(C) 1, 2 and 4 only

(D) 1, 2, 3 and 4

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -By Buying the government securities from the banks and reducing SLR may inject money into the system.

Concept note-2: -When the RBI wants to inject liquidity into economy, it may adopt the following: (1) Buy the government securities from the banks. (2) Enter into reserve repo operations. (3) Raise cash Reserve Ratio.

Concept note-3: -How does RBI manage liquidity? The Reserve Bank of India (RBI), under its Liquidity Adjustment Facility, infuses liquidity in the banking system via repos and sucks it out using reverse repos. The RBI, after assessing liquidity conditions, uses a 14-day variable rate repo and/or reverse repo operation.

Concept note-4: -When RBI wants to reduce liquidity in the banking system, it increases the CRR as it reduces the money supply in the economy.

Concept note-5: -A LAF is a monetary policy tool used in India by the RBI through which it injects or absorbs liquidity into or from the banking system. It was introduced as a part of the outcome of the Narasimham Committee on Banking Sector Reforms of 1998.
