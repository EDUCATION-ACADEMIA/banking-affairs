The insurance company representative who sells policies on behalf of the insurer, is called?

(A) ** Agent

(B) Broker

(C) Applicant

(D) Compulsory

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Description: An insurance agent is a representative who sells the policy on behalf of an insurance company.

Concept note-2: -An agent is a person who acts on behalf of a person or group. In the insurance industry, an agent represents one or more insurers. The agent is responsible for distributing the insurerâ€™s products; their insurance policies. They can represent one or several different insurance companies.

Concept note-3: -By definition, an insurance agent acts on behalf of the insurer it represents, and as such is vested with apparent authority to collect premium payment from an insured.

Concept note-4: -An insurance broker represents you as the client whereas an Insurance Agent represents the company they are employed by as their client, and you become the customer.

Concept note-5: -Insurance agents and insurance brokers can both help you buy an insurance policy. But insurance agents represent the insurance provider that employs them and help sell policies from that single provider.
