Which of the following markets are open to specific investors who buy securities directly from the issuing company?

(A) Tertiary markets

(B) ** Primary markets

(C) Secondary markets

(D) None of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In the primary market, new stocks and bonds are sold to the public for the first time. In a primary market, investors are able to purchase securities directly from the issuer.

Concept note-2: -In the primary market, investors have an option to purchase the shares directly from the company, whereas in the secondary market, the investors buy and sell the securities among themselves.

Concept note-3: -The primary market may also be called the New Issue Market (NIM). In the primary market, securities are directly issued by companies to investors. Securities are issued either by an Initial Public Offer (IPO) or a Further Public Offer (FPO).

Concept note-4: -A broker typically purchases the securities on behalf of an investor in the secondary market. Unlike the primary market, where prices are set before an IPO takes place, prices on the secondary market fluctuate with demand.

Concept note-5: -The primary market is where securities are created. Itâ€™s in this market that firms sell (float) new stocks and bonds to the public for the first time. An initial public offering, or IPO, is an example of a primary market.
