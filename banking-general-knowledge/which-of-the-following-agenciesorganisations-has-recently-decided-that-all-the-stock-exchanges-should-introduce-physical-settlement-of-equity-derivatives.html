Which of the following agencies/organisations has recently decided that all the stock exchanges should introduce physical settlement of equity derivatives?

(A) Reserve Bank of India

(B) Bombay Stock Exchange

(C) Registrar of Companies

(D) ** Securities and Exchange Board of India

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) is the regulatory authority established under the SEBI Act 1992 and is the principal regulator for Stock Exchanges in India. SEBI’s primary functions include protecting investor interests, promoting and regulating the Indian securities markets.

Concept note-2: -The Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE) is India’s largest and earliest securities market. It is also Asia’s first stock exchange.

Concept note-3: -The Securities and Exchange Board of India Act, 1992: It empowers SEBI to register intermediaries like stock brokers, merchant bankers, and portfolio managers, regulate their functioning, impose penalties including suspending/cancelling the registration.
