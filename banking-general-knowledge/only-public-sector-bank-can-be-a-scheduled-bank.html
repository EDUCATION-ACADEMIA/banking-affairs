Only Public Sector Bank can be a scheduled bank.

(A) True

(B) ** False

(C) Partly true

(D) None of these

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Scheduled banks are those banks that are included in the second schedule of the RBI Act, 1934, while the non-scheduled banks are not listed in the second schedule of the RBI Act, 1934. On the basis of ownership, the banks are further divided into–Private Sector Banks, Public Sector Banks, and Foreign Banks.

Concept note-2: -Scheduled Commercial Banks can be divided into: Scheduled Commercial Public Sector Banks. SBI and its associates. Scheduled Commercial Private Sector Banks.

Concept note-3: -As per the latest data released by the RBI, the following are the number of scheduled banks in our country: Scheduled Public Sector Banks – 12. Scheduled Private Sector Banks – 22. Scheduled Small Finance Banks – 11.
