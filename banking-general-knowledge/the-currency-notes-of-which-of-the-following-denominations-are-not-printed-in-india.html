The currency notes of which of the following denominations are not printed in India?

(A) Rs. 5

(B) Rs. 10

(C) Rs. 50

(D) ** Rs. 2500

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The correct option is D ₹ 700 note Which of the following statements is not true with respect to the Indian Currency?

Concept note-2: -The correct answer is Option 1 i.e. Kochi. Currency printing press of India is NOT located in Kochi. There are 4 currency note printing presses in India. Dewas in Madhya Pradesh and Nashik in Maharashtra are controlled by the central government.

Concept note-3: -Banknotes in India are currently being issued in the denomination of ₹10, ₹20, ₹50, ₹100 ₹200, ₹500, and ₹2000. These notes are called banknotes as they are issued by the Reserve Bank of India.

Concept note-4: -Currency Basics Currency-Banknotes in India are currently being issued in the denomination of Rs 10, Rs 20, Rs 50, Rs 100, Rs 200, Rs 500 and Rs 2000. These notes are called banknotes as they are issued by the Reserve Bank of India (Reserve Bank).
