____insurance gives protection to the vehicle owner against damages to his/her vehicle and pays for any Third Party Liability determined as per law against the owner of the vehicle.

(A) ** Motor Insurance

(B) Travel Insurance

(C) Group Insurance

(D) Life Insurance

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Third-party insurance, which is also sometimes referred to as ‘act-only’ insurance is a statutory requirement for all vehicle owners as per the Motor Vehicle Act.

Concept note-2: -First-party insurance covers the damages or losses caused to the policyholder or his/her vehicle. The damages can be sustained during riots, strikes, earthquakes, floods, fire, theft etc. Third-party insurance covers damages or losses caused only to the third party due to an accident.

Concept note-3: -When the third party raises a claim against the insured with respect to an event that has been insured, the insurer undertakes to pay the damages. If the insured is insolvent, then all the rights that he holds in relation to the insurer will be transferred and vested to the third party in the same power.

Concept note-4: -Yes, third party motor insurance is mandatory for two-wheelers and four-wheelers in the country. This mandate has been set by The Motor Vehicles Act, 1988.

Concept note-5: -First-party refers to the insured individual, second-party is the insurance provider, and third party is the person towards whom damages are owed by the first-party in an accident.
