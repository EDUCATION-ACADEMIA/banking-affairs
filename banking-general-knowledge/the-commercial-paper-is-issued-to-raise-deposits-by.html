The commercial paper is issued to raise deposits by____

(A) ** Commercial Banks

(B) Reserve Bank of India

(C) Every Non-Banking company

(D) State Bank of India

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Commercial paper, also called CP, is a short-term debt instrument issued by companies to raise funds generally for a time period up to one year. It is an unsecured money market instrument issued in the form of a promissory note and was introduced in India for the first time in 1990.

Concept note-2: -Commercial paper is an unsecured form of promissory note that pays a fixed rate of interest. It is typically issued by large banks or corporations to cover short-term receivables and meet short-term financial obligations, such as funding for a new project.

Concept note-3: -Commercial paper is an unsecured, short-term debt instrument issued by corporations. Itâ€™s typically used to finance short-term liabilities such as payroll, accounts payable, and inventories. Commercial paper is usually issued at a discount from face value. It reflects prevailing market interest rates.

Concept note-4: -CP may be issued to and held by individuals, banking companies, other corporate bodies registered or incorporated in India and unincorporated bodies, Non-Resident Indians (NRIs) and Foreign Institutional Investors (FIIs).

Concept note-5: -Commercial paper are unsecured promissory notes for a specified amount to be paid at a specified date, and are issued by finance companies, banks, and corporations with excellent credit.
