Which apex body regulates insurance companies in India?

(A) RBI

(B) ** IRDAI

(C) PERDA

(D) SEBI

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -1. Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India (IRDAI), is a statutory body formed under an Act of Parliament, i.e., Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority Act, 1999 (IRDAI Act 1999) for overall supervision and development of the Insurance sector in India.

Concept note-2: -It was constituted by the Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority Act, 1999, an Act of Parliament passed by the Government of India. The agency’s headquarters are in Hyderabad, Telangana, where it moved from Delhi in 2001.

Concept note-3: -Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India or the IRDAI (also referred to as IRDA) is a government body responsible for regulating and developing the insurance industry in India.

Concept note-4: -1) The full form of IRDA is Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India. It is an entity owned by the India Government; it controls India’s insurance industry.

Concept note-5: -The Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India (IRDAI) is an autonomous regulatory body that protects the interests of the policyholder. They oversee the growth of the insurance sector in India, the requirements that different types of insurance policies project, and help maintain a speedy development.
