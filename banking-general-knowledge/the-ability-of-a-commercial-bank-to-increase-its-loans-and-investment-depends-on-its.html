The ability of a commercial Bank to increase its loans and Investment depends on its____

(A) Holding of government securities

(B) Cash position

(C) ** Excess Cash reserves

(D) Outstanding loans and investments

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Federal Reserve can influence the Federal funds rate by buying or selling government bonds. When the Federal Reserve buys bonds, this action increases the supply of excess reserves of banks.

Concept note-2: -Hence the power of the commercial banks to expand deposits through expanding their loans and advances is known as credit creation.

Concept note-3: -Banks with excess reserves lend money to banks with a deficit in reserves. These loans are unsecured so banks only lend to banks they can trust. Usually, smaller banks have the excess reserves to lend while large banks in major metropolitan areas need to borrow.

Concept note-4: -By raising the IOER rate, the central bank gives commercial banks more incentives to hold excess reserves, which reduces the money supply. To conduct an expansionary monetary policy, the central bank can lower the IOER rate. This will lead to commercial banks reducing their excess reserves.
