One rupee notes and coins are issued by

(A) Reserve Bank of India

(B) State Bank of India on behalf of Government of India

(C) ** Ministry of Finance

(D) Finance Minister of Central Government

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Reserve bank of India has the sole right to issue currency notes of various denominations except one rupee note s under Section 22 of Reserve bank of India Act. The one rupee note and coins are issued by ministry of finance and it bears the signature of Finance Secretary.

Concept note-2: -In terms of Section 22 of the Act, Reserve Bank has the sole right to issue banknotes in India.

Concept note-3: -Detailed Solution. The correct answer is Ministry of Finance. The Government of India has the sole right to mint coins. The responsibility for coinage vests with the Government of India in terms of the Coinage Act, 1906 as amended from time to time.

Concept note-4: -Under Section 22 of the Reserve Bank of India Act, RBI has sole right to issue currency notes of various denominations except one rupee note. The One Rupee note is issued by the Ministry of Finance and it bears the signatures of the Finance Secretary, while other notes bear the signature of Governor RBI.

Concept note-5: -The coins are issued for circulation only through the Reserve Bank in terms of the RBI Act. Coins in India are presently being issued in denominations of 10 paise, 20 paise, 25 paise, 50 paise, one rupee, two rupees and five rupees.
