The Holidays for the Banks are declared as per which of the following Act

(A) Reserve bank Act

(B) Banking Regulation Act-1949

(C) ** Negotiable instrument Act-1881

(D) Companies Act-1956

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A bank holiday in India is a public holiday which is declared specially for banks and other financial institutions. Not all public holidays are classified as bank holidays-bank holidays are declared by Central/State Governments/ Union Territory under the Negotiable Instruments (NI) Act, 1881.

Concept note-2: -Section 25 of Negotiable Instruments Act 1881: “When day of maturity is a holiday” When the day on which a promissory note or bill of exchange is at maturity is a public holiday, the instrument shall be deemed to be due on the next preceding business day.

Concept note-3: -Section 138 in The Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881.
