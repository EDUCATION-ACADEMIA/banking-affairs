Which of the following terms indicates a mechanism used by commercial banks for providing credit to the government?

(A) Cash Credit Ratio

(B) Debt Service Obligation

(C) Liquidity Adjustment Facility

(D) ** Statutory Liquidity Ratio

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The correct answer is Statutory Liquidity Ratio. Commercial banks provide long-term credit to the government by investing their funds in government securities and short-term finance by purchasing Treasury Bills.

Concept note-2: -Commercial banks create credit by advancing loans and purchasing securities. They lend money to individuals and businesses out of deposits accepted from the public. However, commercial banks cannot use the entire amount of public deposits for lending purposes.

Concept note-3: -1. Credit to Deposit Ratio: This measures the bank’s total credit in relation to its total deposits in the bank. This helps in analyzing the bank’s liquidity position.

Concept note-4: -RBI has kept 40% as the maximum limit for SLR. SLR is calculated as a percentage of all the deposits held by the bank. Another way to define the SLR meaning is the ratio of a bank’s liquid assets to its net demand and time liabilities. (NDTL).

Concept note-5: -The credit creation process of commercial banks is determined by the amount of initial deposits and the cash reserve ratio.
