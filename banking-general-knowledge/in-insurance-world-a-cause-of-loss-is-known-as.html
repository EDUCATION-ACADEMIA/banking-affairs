In Insurance world, a cause of loss is known as?

(A) ** Peril

(B) Tort

(C) Risk

(D) Negligence

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A specific risk or cause of loss covered by an insurance policy, such as a fire, windstorm, flood, or theft. A named-peril policy covers the policyholder only for the risks named in the policy in contrast to an all-risk policy, which covers all causes of loss except those specifically excluded.

Concept note-2: -A peril is the direct cause of a loss, or the source of the loss. For example, if your house is damaged by a lightning strike, the lightning strike is considered to be the peril. If your house catches on fire, then fire will likely be considered the peril.

Concept note-3: -In the world of insurance, a “peril” is an event or circumstance that results in property damage.

Concept note-4: -Causes of loss are the perils that can bring about or trigger loss or damage.
