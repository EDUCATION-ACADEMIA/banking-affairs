The term “a cheque in the electronic form” is defined in the Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881-under which of the following sections?

(A) ** Section 6

(B) Section 6(l)

(C) Section 6A

(D) None of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Notes: Section 6 of the Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881 (26 of 1881) defines “a cheque in the electronic form” means a cheque drawn in electronic form by using any computer resource and signed in a secure system with digital signature and asymmetric crypto system.

Concept note-2: -Section 6 in The Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881. 1 [ 6 “Cheque”.-A “cheque” is a bill of exchange drawn on a specified banker and not expressed to be payable otherwise than on demand and it includes the electronic image of a truncated cheque and a cheque in the electronic form.

Concept note-3: -Description. A “cheque” is a bill of exchange drawn on a specified banker and not expressed to be payable otherwise than on demand and it includes the electronic image of a truncated cheque and a cheque in the electronic form.

Concept note-4: -Section 13 in The Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881.

Concept note-5: -Section 81A. Application of the Act to electronic cheque and truncated cheque.
