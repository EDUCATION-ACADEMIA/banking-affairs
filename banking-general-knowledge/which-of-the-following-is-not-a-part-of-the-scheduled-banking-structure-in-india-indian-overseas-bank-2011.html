Which of the following is not a part of the scheduled banking structure in India? [Indian Overseas Bank 2011]

(A) State Co-operative Banks

(B) Public Sector Banks

(C) Private Sector Banks

(D) ** Moneylenders

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -All banks which are not included in the second section of the Reserve Bank of India Act, 1934 are Non-scheduled Banks. They are not eligible to borrow from the RBI for normal banking purposes except for emergencies. Scheduled banks are further divided into commercial and cooperative banks.

Concept note-2: -Non-scheduled Bank in India means “a banking company as defined in clause (c) of section 5 of the Banking Regulation Act, 1949, which is not a scheduled bank’.

Concept note-3: -all the RRBs, Cooperative Banks, Indian, and foreign commercial banks belong to the scheduled banks category. Scheduled banks are further classified into commercial banks and cooperative banks.

Concept note-4: -Banks with a reserve capital of less than 5 lakh rupees qualify as non-scheduled banks.
