Which of the following restrictions apply for opening BSBDA by banks for individuals?

(A) Age

(B) Income

(C) Amount

(D) ** None of these

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -No. Banks are advised not to impose restrictions like age and income criteria of the individual for opening BSBDA. 8. Is the ‘Basic Savings Bank Deposit Account’ a part of furthering the Financial Inclusion objectives of banks?

Concept note-2: -All transactions and deposits in the account are free of cost. If the balance exceeds INR 50, 000 or total credit in the account exceeds INR 1, 00, 000 in a year, no further transaction will be permitted until the full KYC procedure is completed. Maximum 4 withdrawals in a month are allowed.

Concept note-3: -Account can be opened by individuals who can open regular savings bank accounts. Customer to give declaration that he/she is not maintaining any BSBDA A/c with other banks while opening the account. 4 per month FREE (including branch, own ATM &other ATM).

Concept note-4: -You may open a new one. An individual is allowed to open only one BSBDA in a bank. If someone holds a regular savings account, it has to be closed within 30 days from opening of the BSBDA, else the bank closes it on its own after 30 days.

Concept note-5: -Political parties These Institutions / Organisations are specifically excluded from opening SB Accounts with Banks and earn interest thereon and hence branches are prohibited from opening such Accounts.
