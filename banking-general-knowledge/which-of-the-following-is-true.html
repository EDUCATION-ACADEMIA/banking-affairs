Which of the following is true?

(A) NBFCs can accept deposits from the public

(B) NBFCs cannot offer deposit schemes to the public

(C) Deposits of NBFCs are insured with DICGC

(D) ** NBFCs can accept deposits from the public if they are registered and permitted by RBI

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -All NBFCs are not entitled to accept public deposits. Only those NBFCs to which the Bank had given a specific authorisation and have an investment grade rating are allowed to accept/ hold public deposits to a limit of 1.5 times of its Net Owned Funds.

Concept note-2: -NBFCs cannot accept demand deposits. Statement 2 is correct.

Concept note-3: -Which entities can legally accept deposits from public? Banks, including co-operative banks, can accept deposits. Non-bank finance companies, which have been issued Certificate of Registration by RBI with a specific licence to accept deposits, are entitled to accept public deposit.

Concept note-4: -Core Investment Companies – (assets are less than 100 crore or public funds not taken) Merchant Banking Companies. Companies that are engaged in the business of stock-broking. Housing Finance Companies.

Concept note-5: -NBFCs do not form part of the payment and settlement system and cannot issue cheques drawn on themselves; iii. the deposit insurance facility of Deposit Insurance and Credit Guarantee Corporation is not available to depositors of NBFCs, unlike in the case of banks. Thus, option 2 is the correct answer.
