What is the maximum period for which domestic term deposits are normally accepted by banks in our country?

(A) 3 years

(B) 5 years

(C) 7 years

(D) ** 10 years

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Domestic & NRO Rupee Term Deposits can be opened for minimum period of 7 days and maximum up to 10 Years. For NRE Term Deposits, minimum period is 1 Year and maximum up to 10 Years.

Concept note-2: -It can range from 7 days to 10 years. The rate of interest for the Fixed Deposit depends on the period for which the funds are locked in. Just like a Recurring Deposit, a Fixed Deposit amount cannot be withdrawn until the maturity period.

Concept note-3: -The advantage of depositing money in an FD is that it will give you higher rates than a savings account. The period of the FD ranges from 7 days to 10 years.

Concept note-4: -15 lakh and above for a minimum maturity period of 7 days and in the case of term deposits of less than Rs. 15 lakh, the minimum maturity period has to be 15 days.

Concept note-5: -The investment tenure of FDs ranges from seven days up to ten years, and it varies across banks. The return on investment is compounded periodically; it may be monthly, quarterly, or annually. Senior citizens are provided with 0.5% higher returns as compared to regular customers.
