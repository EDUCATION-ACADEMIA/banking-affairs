In pursuance of the recommendations of Narsimhan Committee, the RBI has framed new guidelines

(A) ** to govern entry of new private sector banks to make the banking sector more competitive

(B) to reduce the freedom given to banks to rationalize their existing branch network

(C) to set up more foreign exchange banks

(D) to lend more easily for industrial development

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Detailed Solution. The corrent answer is to govern entry of new private sector banks to make the banking sector more competitive.

Concept note-2: -It recommended a three tier banking structure in India through establishment of three large banks with international presence, eight to ten national banks and a large number of regional and local banks.

Concept note-3: -Minimum capital 200 crore, with a commitment to increase to Rs. 300 crore within three years. In order to meet with this requirement, all banks in private sector should have a net worth of Rs 300 crore at all times. Where the net worth declines to level below Rs 300 crore, it should be restored within reasonable time.

Concept note-4: -The Nariman Committee in 1969 recommended a scheme to unite the commercials banks co-operative banks and government and semi-government agencies to work together for the socio-economic growth of the country. The Lead Bank Scheme was also introduced.
