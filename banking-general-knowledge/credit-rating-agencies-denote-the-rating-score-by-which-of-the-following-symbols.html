Credit Rating Agencies denote the rating score by which of the following symbols?

(A) ** Simple Alphanumeric Symbols

(B) Special Symbols

(C) Numerical

(D) None of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Rating is denoted by a simple alphanumeric symbol, for e.g. AA+, A-, etc.

Concept note-2: -A credit rating is always denoted by alphabetical symbols like AA+, A-. Keep in mind that the plus and minus symbols reflect the finer distinctions of your credit rating. A minus symbol does not have a negative connotation. If you rating is ‘AA-’, then it is better than ratings like ‘A+’.

Concept note-3: -How is credit rating expressed/denoted? Credit ratings are generally expressed using a letter-based system or alphanumeric system with symbols, for example A-, AA+, AAA, A1+, A1-etc.

Concept note-4: -A credit rating agency (CRA) is a company that rates debtors on the basis of their ability to pay back their interests and loan amount on time and the probability of them defaulting.

Concept note-5: -Note. CRISIL Ratings may apply modifiers "+” (plus) / “-” (minus) with the rating symbols for the categories ‘CRISIL AA (SO)’ to ‘CRISIL C (SO)’. The modifiers reflect the comparative standing within the category. CRISIL Ratings may assign rating outlooks for ratings from ‘CRISIL AAA’ to ‘CRISIL B’.
