The Union Budget 2018-19 has doubled the allocation on Digital India programme to Rs____crore in 2018-19.

(A) Rs 1231 crore

(B) Rs 16321 crore

(C) ** Rs 3073 crore

(D) Rs 7029 crore

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Budget doubled the allocation on Digital India programme to Rs 3073 crore in 2018-19. To further Broadband access in villages, the Government proposes to set up five lakh wi-fi hotspots to provide net connectivity to five crore rural citizens.

Concept note-2: -In 2018-19, the Department of School Education and Literacy has been allocated Rs 50, 000 crore, a 6.4% increase over the revised estimates of 2017-18. In 2017-18, the Department spent Rs 650 crore over the budgeted amount (Rs 46, 356 crore).

Concept note-3: -The Union Budget has estimated gross tax revenue to grow by 16.7 per cent in 2018-19 to 12.1 per cent of GDP (Annex IV). Net of devolution of tax revenues to States, net tax revenue is budgeted to grow by 16.6 per cent to 7.9 per cent of GDP.

Concept note-4: -Union Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman on Sunday announced an outlay of Rs 4, 795.24 crore for the Digital India Programme as part of the Budget 2023-24 on Wednesday.

Concept note-5: -Finance Minister Shri Arun Jaitley presents general Budget 2018-19 in Parliament. Government says, a series of structural reforms will propel India among the fastest growing economies of the world.
