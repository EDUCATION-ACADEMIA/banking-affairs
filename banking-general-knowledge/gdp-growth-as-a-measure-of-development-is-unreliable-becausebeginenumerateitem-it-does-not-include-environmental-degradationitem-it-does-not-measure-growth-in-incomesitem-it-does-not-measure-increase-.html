GDP growth as a measure of development is unreliable because

1. :

it does not include environmental degradation.

2. :

it does not measure growth in incomes.

3. :

it does not measure increase in jobs.

Select the correct answer using the codes given below:

(A) 2 and 3

(B) 1 and 2

(C) ** 1 and 3

(D) Only 3

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Many key goods, including peacefulness, environmental protection or family bonding, are not measured in GDP because they do not involve transactions. In fact, GDP includes pollution, crime, the health costs of cigarettes and environmental disasters as ‘growth’ because they generate spending.

Concept note-2: -Considering limitations of GDP-based growth accounting in capturing the impact of climate change, the significance of Green GDP which adjusts for environmental deterioration and waning natural resources into estimates of national income accounts, has grown manifold.

Concept note-3: -Which of the following is considered to be a problem in using GDP as a measure of national well-being? GDP is not adjusted to account for depletion of natural resources.
