Banks are authorized to sell third party products. Which are these products?

(A) ** Mutual funds

(B) Term deposits

(C) Credit cards

(D) Demand Draft

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -List of third-party products sold by banks: 1. Insurance Products 2. Mutual Funds 3. Government Bonds 4.

Concept note-2: -Third Party Products means any products manufactured by or provided by a party other than NACR, and may include, without limitation, products ordered by CUSTOMER from third parties pursuant to NACR’s recommendations.

Concept note-3: -Mutual funds sell their products through distributors and directly. Third party distributors are mostly wealth managers, independent firms, bank branches or individuals. While wealth management units of banks sell schemes of all mutual funds, its branches mostly push products owned by the subsidiary mutual funds.

Concept note-4: -(TPP) Third Party Providers are organisations or natural persons that use APIs developed to Standards to access customer’s accounts, in order to provide account information services and/or to initiate payments.
