The Vedic word Kusidin refers to____?

(A) A banker

(B) ** An usurer

(C) A farmer

(D) A trader

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Notes: The Vedic word Kusidin refers to an usurer. Usury was prevalent in India since ancient times.

Concept note-2: -The Vedic word Kusidin refers to a usurer. This term is also found in Manusmriti.

Concept note-3: -The Vedic word Kusidin refers to an usurer. Manusmriti condemns usury and calls it an acceptable means of acquiring wealth.

Concept note-4: -The Early Vedic period is also known as the Rig Vedic period. In this period, the Indo-Aryans (c. 1750 BCE to 1000 BCE) believed mostly in pastoral activities, limited agriculture with a smaller economy.

Concept note-5: -The groups of the villages belonged to a clan (Vis) and many clans made a community called Jana.
