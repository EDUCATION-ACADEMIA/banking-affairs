According to the data released by Central Statistics Office (CSO), IIP has grew at 6.6% in July on the back of good performance by the manufacturing sector and higher offtake of capital goods and consumer durables. What is the full form of IIP?

(A) Index of Industrial Power

(B) ** Index of Industrial Production

(C) Index of Industrial Purchasing

(D) Index of Industrial Particular

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -For the month of July 2022, the Quick Estimates of Index of Industrial Production (IIP) with base 2011-12 stands at 134.6. The Indices of Industrial Production for the Mining, Manufacturing and Electricity sectors for the month of July 2022 stand at 101.1, 135.2 and 188.9 respectively.

Concept note-2: -The Index of Industrial Production (IIP) grew by 1 per cent in November 2021. As per the IIP data released by the National Statistical Office (NSO), the manufacturing sectorâ€™s output grew by 6.1% in November 2022. The mining output rose by 9.7% and power generation increased by 12.7% during the month under review.

Concept note-3: -Industrial production in India increased 4.3% year-on-year in December of 2022, easing from an upwardly revised 7.3% rise in November, and slightly below market forecasts of a 4.5% gain. Electricity production recorded the biggest increase (10.4%), followed by mining (9.8%) and manufacturing (2.6%).

Concept note-4: -The Index of Industrial Production (IIP) had grown 4.2% in October 2021 and by 3.1% in September 2022. IIP contracted to 129.6 in October from 133.5 in September.
