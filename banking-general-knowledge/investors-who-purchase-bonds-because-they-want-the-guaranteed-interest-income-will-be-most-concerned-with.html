Investors who purchase bonds because they want the guaranteed interest income will be most concerned with

(A) liquidity

(B) maturity

(C) par value

(D) ** yield

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Investors buy bonds because: They provide a predictable income stream. Typically, bonds pay interest twice a year. If the bonds are held to maturity, bondholders get back the entire principal, so bonds are a way to preserve capital while investing.

Concept note-2: -Interest rate risk directly affects the values of fixed income securities. Since interest rates and bond prices are inversely related, the risk associated with a rise in interest rates causes bond prices to fall and vice versa. Interest rate risk affects the prices of bonds, and all bondholders face this type of risk.
