(C) Odisha

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Hazaribagh Wildlife Sanctuary (earlier called Hazaribagh National Park) is a wildlife sanctuary in Jharkhand, India, about 55 miles (89 km) north of Ranchi. It was established in 1955.

Concept note-2: -Hazaribag Wildlife Sanctuary, Hazaribag also spelled Hazaribagh, national park, north-central Jharkhand state, northeastern India. The sanctuary is situated on a hilly plateau at an average elevation of 2, 000 feet (600 metres), about 55 miles (90 km) north of Ranchi, the state capital.

Concept note-3: -Hazaribagh National Park, Hazaribagh Overview It was established in 1954 with an objective of conserving wildlife and providing a home for rare and endangered species of flora and fauna. The landscape is full of steep hills, spectacular countryside, thick tropical forest and lovely meadows.

Concept note-4: -HAZARIBAGH NATIONAL PARK Nestling in low hilly terrain, at an average altitude of 615 m the park has an abundance of wild animals like the wild boar, sambar, nilgai, chital, sloth bear, tiger and panther.
