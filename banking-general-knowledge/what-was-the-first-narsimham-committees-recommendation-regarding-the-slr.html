What was the first Narsimham Committee’s recommendation regarding the SLR?

(A) to bring it down from 38.5% to 25%

(B) to bring it up from 38.5% to 45%

(C) ** to bring it down from 38.5% to 25%

(D) None of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The SLR was 38.5%, and CRR was 15%. To increase the bank’s productivity rates, the committee recommended reducing these high proportions. Accordingly, they suggested reducing SLR rates from 38.5% to 25% and CRR from 15% to 3-5%.

Concept note-2: -It recommended a three tier banking structure in India through establishment of three large banks with international presence, eight to ten national banks and a large number of regional and local banks. This proposal had been severely criticized by the RBI employees union.

Concept note-3: -Recommendations of Narasimhan Committee A phased reduction in statutory liquidity ratio. Phased achievement of 8% capital adequacy ratio. Abolition of branch licensing policy. Proper classification of assets and full disclosure of accounts of banks and financial institutions.

Concept note-4: -Narasimham Committee for Financial Sector Reforms (1991) has suggested a reduction in SLR, CRR, and priority sector financing reducing it from 40% to 10%.
