Under which of the following methods of depreciation, amount of depreciation varies every year?

(A) ** Written Down Value Method

(B) Straight Line Method

(C) Amount of depreciation does not vary on year to year basis

(D) Either A or B

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Under Reducing Balance Method of depreciation amount of depreciation changes every year.

Concept note-2: -Depreciation Methods Written-down value can be calculated by a method of depreciation that is sometimes called the diminishing balance method. This accounting technique reduces the value of an asset by a set percentage each year.

Concept note-3: -Under the diminishing balance method of providing depreciation, it decreases every year.

Concept note-4: -Under written down value method, the depreciation is calculated at a certain fixed percentage each year on the decreasing book value.

Concept note-5: -Straight-Line Depreciation Method In straight-line depreciation, the expense amount is the same every year over the useful life of the asset.
