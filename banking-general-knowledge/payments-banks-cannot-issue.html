Payments banks cannot issue

(A) ATM

(B) Debit cards

(C) ** credit cards

(D) Both A and C

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Definition: A payments bank is like any other bank, but operating on a smaller scale without involving any credit risk. In simple words, it can carry out most banking operations but can’t advance loans or issue credit cards.

Concept note-2: -No, small finance banks cannot issue credit cards but they are allowed to issue debit cards.

Concept note-3: -Issuance of ATM / Debit Cards. Payments banks, however, cannot issue credit cards.

Concept note-4: -Payments bank comes under a differentiated bank licence since it cannot offer all the services that a commercial bank offers. In particular, a payments bank cannot lend. It can take deposits upto ₹1 lakh per account and it can issue debit cards but not credit cards.

Concept note-5: -Payments banks can issue ATM cards or debit cards and provide online or mobile banking.
