I = P x R x T, what does T stand for?

(A) ** Time in years

(B) Time in months

(C) Time in days

(D) Interest Rate

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -= P × R × T, where P = Principal, R = Rate of Interest in % per annum, and T = Time, usually calculated as the number of years.

Concept note-2: -t = number of years the amount is deposited or borrowed for. A = amount of money accumulated after n years, including interest.

Concept note-3: -Time = (100 × Interest)/(Principal × Rate) Therefore, Time (T) = 5 years.

Concept note-4: -The general time formula for any task is given as [Time = Distance ÷ Speed]. SI unit of time is seconds (s).

Concept note-5: -In the simple-interest formula I = Prt, the variable I stands for the interest on the original investment, P stands for the amount of the original investment (called the “principal"), r is the interest rate (expressed in decimal form), and t is the time. For annual interest, the time t must be in years.
