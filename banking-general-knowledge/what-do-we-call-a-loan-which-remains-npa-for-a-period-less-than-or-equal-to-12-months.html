What do we call a loan which remains NPA for a period less than or equal to 12 months?

(A) Doubtful asset

(B) ** Sub-standard Asset

(C) Loss

(D) None of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -An asset is classified as a sub-standard asset if it remains as an NPA for a period less than or equal to 12 months. An asset is classified as a doubtful asset if it remained as an NPA for more than 12 months.

Concept note-2: -Substandard assets: Assets which has remained NPA for a period less than or equal to 12 months.

Concept note-3: -3 Doubtful Assets With effect from March 31, 2005, an asset is required to be classified as doubtful, if it has remained NPA for more than 12 months. For Tier I banks, the 12-month period of classification of a substandard asset in doubtful category is effective from April 1, 2009.

Concept note-4: -1. Standard Assets. They are NPAs that have been past due for anywhere from 90 days to 12 months, with a normal risk level.
