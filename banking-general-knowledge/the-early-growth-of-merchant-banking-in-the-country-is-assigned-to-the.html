The early growth of merchant banking in the country is assigned to the

(A) ** Foreign exchange regulations act 1973

(B) fema

(C) Income tax act

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Foreign Exchange Regulation Act (FERA) was legislation passed in India in 1973 that imposed strict regulations on certain kinds of payments, the dealings in foreign exchange (forex) and securities and the transactions which had an indirect impact on the foreign exchange and the import and export of currency.

Concept note-2: -Notes: The early growth of merchant banking in the country is assigned to Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1973.

Concept note-3: -1. This Act may be called the Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1973. 2. It extends to the whole of India.
