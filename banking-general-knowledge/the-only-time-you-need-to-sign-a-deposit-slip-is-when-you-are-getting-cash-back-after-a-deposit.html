The only time you need to sign a deposit slip is when you are getting cash back after a deposit.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -You typically only need to sign a deposit slip when you want cash back from your deposit. If you’re only depositing funds, you don’t need to sign the deposit slip. If you’re making a deposit through an ATM, you may not need a deposit slip at all, so you won’t need to sign anything.

Concept note-2: -When you deposit cash at a bank or credit union, you typically need to use a deposit slip. That’s simply a slip of paper that tells the teller where to put the money. Write your name and account number on the deposit slip (deposit slips are usually available at the lobby or drive-through).

Concept note-3: -A check must be endorsed on the back for it to be valid for deposit. So, always sign your name in the blank space next to the X just before you bring it to the Bank. Note: You can deposit at a Bank location, through our mobile app, or at an ATM. 2.

Concept note-4: -The deposit slip provides proof and acknowledgment that the bank has received the funds. Once the teller has accepted the deposit for processing, they normally sign the deposit slip and place the bank’s official stamp on both the original and duplicate deposit slips.
