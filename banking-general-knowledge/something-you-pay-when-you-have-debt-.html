Something you pay when you have debt *

(A) FICA

(B) Taxes

(C) ** Interest

(D) Social Security

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Interest effects the overall price you pay after your loan is completely paid off. For example, if you borrow $100 with a 5% interest rate, you will pay $105 dollars back to the lender you borrowed from. The lender will make $5 in profit.

Concept note-2: -The interest rate is the amount a lender charges a borrower and is a percentage of the principal-the amount loaned. The interest rate on a loan is typically noted on an annual basis known as the annual percentage rate (APR).

Concept note-3: -the act of paying back money that you have borrowed: make/meet debt payments Many people are struggling to meet their debt payments.

Concept note-4: -Reasons for Paying Interest Lenders demand that borrowers pay interest for several important reasons. First, when people lend money, they can no longer use this money to fund their own purchases. The payment of interest makes up for this inconvenience. Second, a borrower may default on the loan.
