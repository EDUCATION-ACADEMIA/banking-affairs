When the Federal Reserve wants to encourage the economy to grow, what does it do with the money supply?

(A) ** increase the money supply

(B) decrease the money supply

(C) sell all of its assets

(D) issue more government securities

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Fed can influence the money supply by modifying reserve requirements, which generally refers to the amount of funds banks must hold against deposits in bank accounts. By lowering the reserve requirements, banks are able to loan more money, which increases the overall supply of money in the economy.

Concept note-2: -Conducting monetary policy If the Fed, for example, buys or borrows Treasury bills from commercial banks, the central bank will add cash to the accounts, called reserves, that banks are required keep with it. That expands the money supply.

Concept note-3: -It is responsible for managing monetary policy and regulating the financial system. It does this by setting interest rates, influencing the supply of money in the economy, and, in recent years, making trillions of dollars in asset purchases to boost financial markets.

Concept note-4: -If the Fed increases the interest rate of reserves, banks will want to keep higher reserves. This will reduce the money multiplier as higher reserves effectively mean a higher reserve ratio. A fall in the money multiplier will reduce the loans given out by banks, reducing the money supply.
