BANKING GENERAL KNOWLEDGE

[SOURCES]
Drawing, accepting, making or issuing of any promissory note, hundi or bill of exchange expressed to be payable to bearer on demand by a person other than the Reserve Bank of India or the Central Government is prohibited under

(A) Banking Regulation Act, 1949

(B) ** Section 31 (1) of the Reserve Bank of India Act, 1934

(C) Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881

(D) Indian Contract Act, 1872

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -(1) A “negotiable instrument” means a promissory note, bill of exchange or cheque payable either to order or to bearer.

Concept note-2: -A promissory note cannot be made payable to the bearer, no matter whether it is payable on demand or after a certain time. 2. A bill of exchange cannot be made payable to the bearer on demand though it can be made payable to the bearer after a certain time.

Concept note-3: -(2) Notwithstanding anything contained in the Negotiable Instrument Act, 1881 (26 of 1881), no person in [India] other than the Bank or, as expressly authorised by this Act, the Central Government shall make or issue any promissory note expressed to be payable to the bearer of the instrument.

Concept note-4: -Instruments payable on demand.-A promissory note or bill of exchange, in which no time for payment is specified, and a cheque, are payable on demand.
