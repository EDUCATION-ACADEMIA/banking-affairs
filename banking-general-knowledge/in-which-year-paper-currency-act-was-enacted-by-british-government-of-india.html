Concept note-3: -The Paper Currency Act of 1861 conferred upon Government of India the monopoly of Note Issue bringing to an end note issues of Private and Presidency Banks.

Concept note-4: -An Act to provide for a Government Paper Currency. An Act to enable the Banks of Bengal, Madras and Bombay to enter into arrangements with the Government, for managing the issue, payment and exchange of Government Currency Notes and certain business hitherto transacted by the Government Treasuries.

Concept note-5: -The expansionist strategies of the East India Company led Bengal to a scarcity of gold and silver bullion. Sensing an emerging credit crisis, the British official introduced paper currency in India.
