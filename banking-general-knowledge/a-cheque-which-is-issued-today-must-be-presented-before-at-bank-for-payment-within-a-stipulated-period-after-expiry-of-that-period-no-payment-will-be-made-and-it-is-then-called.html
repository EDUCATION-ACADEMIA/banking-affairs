A cheque which is issued today must be presented before at bank for payment within a stipulated period. After expiry of that period, no payment will be made and it is then called.

(A) Mutilated Cheque

(B) ** Stale cheque

(C) Crossed cheque

(D) Open cheque

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Cheque Bounce Notice under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act. A cheque bounce notice is issued under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act when a cheque bounces due to insufficient funds in the drawersâ€™ account to make the cheque amount payment.

Concept note-2: -Definition of Stale Cheque In finer terms, a stale cheque is one that is presented for payment by the payee, at the drawee bank for encashment, but not accepted by the bank, because it is presented after the reasonable period, i.e. three months (earlier it was six months) of its date of payment.

Concept note-3: -It can only be cashed after the date specified by the payer. The post-dated cheque can be valid after the mentioned date but not before it. Hence, even if it is presented to the bank, the bank will not process it until the mentioned date.

Concept note-4: -Cheques that are issued by business but not yet presented to bank are known as Unpresented Cheques.
