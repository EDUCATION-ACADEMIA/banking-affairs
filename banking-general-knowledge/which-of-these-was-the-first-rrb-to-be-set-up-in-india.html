Which of these was the first RRB to be set up in India?

(A) Gaur Gramin Bank

(B) ** Prathama Bank

(C) Arunachal Pradesh Rural Bank

(D) Himachal Gramin Bank

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Prathama Bank, with head office in Moradabad, Uttar Pradesh was the first RRB. It was sponsored by Syndicate Bank and had an authorised capital of Rs. 5 crore.

Concept note-2: -Regional Rural banks (RRBs) in India were established by Government of India, under provisions of RRBs Act 1976, enacted by the Indian Parliament.

Concept note-3: -Notes: Gour Gramin Bank, set up in Malda(West Bengal) was the first RRB in the Eastern Region of India.

Concept note-4: -Regional Rural Banks were establishedunder the provisions of an Ordinance passed on September 1975 and the RRB Act.

Concept note-5: -From a modest beginning of 6 RRBs with 17 branches covering 12 districts in December, 1975 the number of RRBs increased to 196 RRBs with 14, 446 branches in 1991 operating in 518 districts across the country.
