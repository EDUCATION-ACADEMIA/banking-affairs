Which of the following statements is incorrect about negotiable instruments?

(A) Pay A or Order

(B) Pay to Order to A

(C) Pay A and B

(D) ** Pay A only

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -An instrument can be negotiated even if it is conditional on some future event, or if the amount to be paid will be determined at some future date-this is a false statement about the negotiable instrument.

Concept note-2: -A crossed cheque can only be paid in account & hence it is not a negotiable instrument.

Concept note-3: -Negotiable instruments are unconditional orders or promise to pay, and include checks, drafts, bearer bonds, some certificates of deposit, promissory notes etc. are written contracts whose benefit could be passed on from its original holder to a new holder.

Concept note-4: -According to the Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881 there are just three types of negotiable instruments i.e., promissory note, bill of exchange and cheque.
