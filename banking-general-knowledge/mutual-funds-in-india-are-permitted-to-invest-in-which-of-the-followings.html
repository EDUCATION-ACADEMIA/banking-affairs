Mutual funds in India are permitted to invest in which of the followings?

(A) Securities

(B) Securities and Gold

(C) Securities other than Real Estate

(D) ** Securities, Gold and Real Estate

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Notes: Mutual funds in India are permitted to invest in Securities, Gold and Real Estate. A mutual fund is prohibited from investing in any unlisted security or a security issued through private placement by an associate or a group company of the sponsor.

Concept note-2: -If you are interested in the real estate sector and believe that this sector will undergo growth in the near future and want to invest in it, then Real Estate Mutual Funds in India are ideal for you. Here, we will talk about these funds, along with their features and the benefits they offer.

Concept note-3: -A mutual fund is a company that pools money from many investors and invests the money in securities such as stocks, bonds, and short-term debt. The combined holdings of the mutual fund are known as its portfolio. Investors buy shares in mutual funds.

Concept note-4: -Mutual funds in real estate are funds that invest directly in commercial lands, residential complexes, stocks of real estate companies, corporate properties, and agricultural lands. Mutual fund real estate has options to invest directly in property or indirectly by investing in REITs (Real estate investment trusts).

Concept note-5: -REIT mutual funds are a type of sector fund that invests in securities from real estate sectors. The funds generated from investment act as capital to support a real estate company in building a property.
