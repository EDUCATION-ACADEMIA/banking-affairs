Which of the following interest rates are not fixed by RBI and left to the discretion of the Bank concerned?

1. :

Saving Bank deposits

2. :

DRI Advances

3. :

Ceilling rates on FCNR/NRE deposit accounts

4. :

Ceiling rates on export credit facilities

(A) 1 and 4

(B) 1 and 3

(C) Only 1

(D) ** Only 4

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The current Repo Rate as fixed by the RBI is 5.90%. After the latest hike in the repo rate on 7 December 2022, the Marginal Standing Facility (MSF) Rate rose to 6.15% from 5.65% and currently stands at 6.50%.

Concept note-2: -With effect from October 18, 1994, RBI has deregulated the interest rates on advances above Rs. 2 lakh and the rates of interest on such advances are determined by the banks themselves subject to BPLR and Spread guidelines. For credit limits up to Rs. 2 lakh, banks should charge interest not exceeding their BPLR.

Concept note-3: -Bank of India offers FD interest rates of 3.00%-7.05% p.a. to regular citizens and 3.00%-7.55% p.a. to senior citizens on tenures ranging from 7 days to 10 years. The interest rate on Bank of India Tax Saving FDs is 6.00% p.a. for the general public and 6.75% p.a.

Concept note-4: -The new FD rule is related to interest being paid on unclaimed or overdue FDs. As per the new rule, if a term deposit matures and proceeds are unpaid, the unclaimed amount will now earn a lesser interest rate.
