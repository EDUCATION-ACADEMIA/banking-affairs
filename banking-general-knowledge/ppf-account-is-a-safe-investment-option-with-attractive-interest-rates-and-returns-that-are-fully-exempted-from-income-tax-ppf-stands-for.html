PPF account is a safe investment option with attractive interest rates and returns that are fully exempted from Income Tax. PPF stands for

(A) Public Provident Follow

(B) Public Provident Firm

(C) ** Public Provident Fund

(D) Public Personal Fund

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -7.1% per annum. Rs 1.5 lakh per annum. Public Provident Fund (PPF) scheme is a long-term investment option that offers an attractive rate of interest and returns on the amount invested. The interest earned and the returns are not taxable under Income Tax.

Concept note-2: -For long-term, risk-free investments, the Public Provident Fund (PPF) remains one of the most preferred instruments among investors. Tax-saving benefits and tax-free returns make PPF an ideal investment for long-term financial goals.

Concept note-3: -Deposits to a PPF account are exempted from the taxation up to a maximum of Rs. 1.5 lakh in a FY under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act, 1961. The second exemption is on the interest earned from your PPF deposits.
