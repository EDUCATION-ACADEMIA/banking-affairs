The European Union has adopted which of the following as a common currency?

(A) Dollar

(B) Dinar

(C) Yen

(D) ** Euro

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Detailed Solution. The correct answer is European Union. Euro, monetary unit and currency of the European Union (EU). It was introduced as a noncash monetary unit in 1999, and currency notes and coins appeared in participating countries on January 1, 2002.

Concept note-2: -An economic and monetary union (EMU) was a recurring ambition for the European Union from the late 1960s onwards. EMU involves coordinating economic and fiscal policies, a common monetary policy, and a common currency, the euro.

Concept note-3: -You can use the euro in 20 EU countries: Austria, Belgium, Croatia, Cyprus, Estonia, Finland, France, Germany, Greece, Ireland, Italy, Latvia, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Malta, the Netherlands, Portugal, Slovakia, Slovenia and Spain. Discover more about the euro, which countries use it and the exchange rates.

Concept note-4: -European Union – Economy Euro is used as the common currency in the Eurozone. Euro is the 2nd most traded currency in the world.

Concept note-5: -On 1 January 1999, 11 EU countries launched the euro as their new common currency. The euro was initially an electronic currency. Euro banknotes and coins were introduced three years later. Today, the euro is the currency of 20 EU countries and over 346 million Europeans.
