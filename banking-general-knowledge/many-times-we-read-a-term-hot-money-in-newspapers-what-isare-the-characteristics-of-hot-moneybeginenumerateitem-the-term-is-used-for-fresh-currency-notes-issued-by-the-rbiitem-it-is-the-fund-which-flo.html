Many times we read a term .Hot Money. in newspapers. What is/are the characteristics of Hot money?

1. :

The term is used for fresh currency notes issued by the RBI.

2. :

It is the fund which flows in the market to take advantage of high interest rates.

3. :

It is the fund which is thrown in the market to create imbalance in the stock markets.

(A) Only 1

(B) ** Only 2

(C) Only 3

(D) All the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -What is/are the characteristics of Hot money? (1) The term is used for fresh currency notes issued by the RBI. (2) It is the fund that flows in the market to take advantage of high-interest rates. (3) It is the fund which is thrown in the market to create an imbalance in the stock markets.

Concept note-2: -"Hot money” refers to funds that are controlled by investors who actively seek short-term returns. These investors scan the market for short-term, high interest rate investment opportunities. A typical short-term investment opportunity that often attracts “hot money” is the certificate of deposit (CD).

Concept note-3: -Types of hot money As mentioned above, capital in the following form could be considered hot money: Short-term foreign portfolio investments, including investments in equities, bonds and financial derivatives. Short-term foreign bank loans. Foreign bank loans with short term investment horizon.

Concept note-4: -Hot money refers to funds that are quickly mobile, searching for immediate profit that travels across the border. Hence, hot money is a part of international capital.
