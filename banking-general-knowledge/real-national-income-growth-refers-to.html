Real national income growth refers to

(A) ** national income growth adjusted for inflation

(B) national income growth adjusted for population growth

(C) national income growth adjusted for depreciation rate

(D) national income growth adjusted for saving growth

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -2. Economic growth means increase in real national income.

Concept note-2: -Key Takeaways Real gross domestic product is an inflation-adjusted measure that reflects the value of all goods and services produced by an economy in a given year. Real GDP is expressed in base-year prices and is often referred to as constant price, inflation-corrected, or constant dollar GDP.

Concept note-3: -Real income is the income of individuals or nations after adjusting for inflation. It is calculated by dividing nominal income by the price level.

Concept note-4: -The value of national income adjusted for inflation is called Real national income. This is adjusted for inflation which is calculated from a reference year which is also called as base year.
