When signing up for a new checking account you answer “Yes” to receive overdraft protection. On this day, you have $10 in your account. You go out and use your debit card to buy lunch for $12, a movie ticket for $12 and dinner for $15. What is likely to be the outcome resulting from these transactions?

(A) Your account would be closed

(B) ** You will likely be charged an overdraft fee for EACH transaction and also will need to repay the bank for the amount overdrawn ($29)

(C) Because you requested overdraft protection, the bank will not allow you to overdraw your account, so your debit card will be denied in these 3 instances

(D) The bank will provide you five days to add funds to your account to cover the overdraft and no fees will be assessed

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Overdraft protection is an agreement with the bank or financial institution to cover overdrafts on a checking account. This service typically involves a fee and is generally limited to a preset maximum amount.

Concept note-2: -Whether you apply for a bank account online or in person, you’ll need a government-issued ID and personal details, such as your Social Security number, on hand. You might also be asked to fund your account with an initial deposit.

Concept note-3: -You’ll need to provide personal information, such as your name, address and birthday, plus your social security number. In some cases, the bank may run a credit check, but it will likely be a soft pull that doesn’t hurt your credit. You can double check the terms before opening an account to verify this.

Concept note-4: -Overdraft fees occur when you don’t have enough money in your account to cover your transactions. The cost for overdraft fees varies by bank, but they may cost around $35 per transaction. These fees can add up quickly and can have ripple effects that are costly.
