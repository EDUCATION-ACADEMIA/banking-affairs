The market which expects the prices of shares go up in the market is called

(A) Depressed market

(B) ** Bull market

(C) Duck market

(D) Bear market

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A bull market is a financial market characterized by rising prices and investor optimism. It is most commonly used to refer to the stock market, but can also refer to the bond, real estate, currency, and commodity markets.

Concept note-2: -A bull market occurs when securities are on the rise, while a bear market occurs when securities fall for a sustained period of time.

Concept note-3: -That is, a bull will thrust its horns up into the air, while a bear will swipe down. These actions were then related metaphorically to the movement of a market. If the trend was up, it was considered a bull market. If the trend was down, it was a bear market.

Concept note-4: -A bull market occurs when asset prices rise significantly over a sustained period. While analysts often use the term “bull market” to discuss stocks and the stock market, the term can be used for any asset – bonds, real estate, commodities or even cryptocurrency – that is rising over time.

Concept note-5: -A bull market is a market that is on the rise and where the conditions of the economy are generally favorable. A bear market exists in an economy that is receding and where most stocks are declining in value.
