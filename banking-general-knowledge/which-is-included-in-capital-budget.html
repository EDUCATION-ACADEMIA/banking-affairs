Which is included in Capital budget?

(A) ** Income received from public borrowings.

(B) Income received from non-tax sources

(C) All of the above

(D) None of these

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Capital Budget consists of capital receipts (like disinvestment, borrowing, loans from public or foreign governments, Reserve Bank of India, etc) and capital expenditure (like expenditure on development of machinery, health facilities, etc).

Concept note-2: -Capital payments consist of capital expenditure on acquisition of assets like land, buildings, machinery, and equipment, as also investments in shares, loans and advances granted by the Central government to state and Union Territory governments, government companies, corporations and other parties.

Concept note-3: -Sale of shares of a Public Sector Undertaking (PSU) to X Limited (Private Company) is a capital receipt in the Government Budget.

Concept note-4: -Borrowing leads to cash inflow, which means it is a receipt. Since borrowing increases the liability for the government, it is listed as capital receipt.
