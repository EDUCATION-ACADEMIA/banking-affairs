The payment of interest on a promissory note on which the rate of interest is not mentioned, is regulated as per

(A) Section 80 of Contract Act

(B) ** Section 80 of Negotiable Instrument Act

(C) Section 21 of RBI Act

(D) Section 21 of Negotiable Instrument Act

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A crossed cheque can only be paid in account & hence it is not a negotiable instrument.

Concept note-2: -A “promissory note” is an instrument in writing (not being a bank-note or a currency-note) containing an unconditional undertaking, signed by the maker, to pay a certain sum of money only to, or to the order of, a certain person, or to the bearer of the instrument.
