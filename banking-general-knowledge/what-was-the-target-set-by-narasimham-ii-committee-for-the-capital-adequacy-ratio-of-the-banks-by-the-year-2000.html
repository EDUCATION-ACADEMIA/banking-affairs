What was the target set by Narasimham-II Committee for the capital adequacy ratio of the banks by the year 2000?

(A) 8%

(B) ** 9%

(C) 10%

(D) 11%

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Commercial banks are currently required to maintain a minimum CRAR of 8%. As per the recommendations of the Committee on Banking Sector Reforms (Narsimham Committee II) made in 1998, this has to be increased from 8% to 10% in a phased manner with an intermediate target of 9% by March 2000.

Concept note-2: -The RBI targeted to bring the capital adequacy ratio to 9% by March 2001.

Concept note-3: -Accordingly, they suggested reducing SLR rates from 38.5% to 25% and CRR from 15% to 3-5%. The Government of India implemented credit programmes that compelled banks to set aside funds for the needy and poor sectors at decreased rates.
