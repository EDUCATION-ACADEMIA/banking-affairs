Under which act NBFCs are incorporated?

(A) Banking Regulation Act, 1949

(B) ** Companies Act, 1956

(C) RBI Act 1934

(D) SBI Act 1955

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A Non–Banking Financial Corporation is a company incorporated under the Companies Act 2013 or 1956. According to section 45-I (c) of the RBI Act, a Non–Banking Company carrying on the business of a financial institution will be an NBFC.

Concept note-2: -In terms of Section 45-IA of the RBI Act, 1934 it is mandatory for a company to obtain Certificate of Registration (CoR) from Reserve Bank of India (RBI) before commencing or to carry on business of a non-banking financial institution.

Concept note-3: -The Reserve Bank of India Act, 1934 amended on 1 December 1964 by Reserve Bank Amendment Act, 1963. In this new ‘Chapter III-B’ introduced to Regulate ‘Deposit Accepting’ NBFCs.
