This act that was passed in 2002, ensures more accurate disclosure of a company’s financial information:

(A) The Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act

(B) The Truth in Lending Act

(C) ** The Sabanes-Oxley Act

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Sarbanes-Oxley Act of 2002 is a federal law that established sweeping auditing and financial regulations for public companies. Lawmakers created the legislation to help protect shareholders, employees and the public from accounting errors and fraudulent financial practices.

Concept note-2: -The Sarbanes-Oxley Act of 2002 mandates that audit committees be directly responsible for the oversight of the engagement of the company’s independent auditor, and the Securities and Exchange Commission (the Commission) rules are designed to ensure that auditors are independent of their audit clients.
