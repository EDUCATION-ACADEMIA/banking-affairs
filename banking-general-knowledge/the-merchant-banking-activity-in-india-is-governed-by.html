The merchant banking activity in India is governed by

(A) ** SEBI

(B) RBI

(C) IRDA

(D) All of these

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Securities and Exchange Board of India (Merchant Bankers) Regulations, 1992, was amended on December 09, 1997 by the SEBI (Merchant Bankers) Amendment Regulations, 1997, vide. S.O. 837(E), which prohibited registered merchant bankers from undertaking any activity other than that in the securities market.

Concept note-2: -Yes. As per regulation 9A of SEBI (Merchant Bankers) Regulations, 1992, change in control shall affect the obligation to obtain a fresh registration under section 12 of the SEBI Act, 1992. Further, a Merchant Banker is required to obtain fresh certificate of registration within a period of six months.

Concept note-3: -SEBI’s main role in the Indian financial system is to regulate that the Indian stock markets in an orderly manner. SEBI was formed to protect the interests of investors and traders in the Indian stock market.
