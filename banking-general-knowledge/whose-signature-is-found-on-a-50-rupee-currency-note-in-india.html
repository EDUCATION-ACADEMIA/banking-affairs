Whose signature is found on a 50 rupee currency note in India?

(A) President of India

(B) ** Governor, RBI

(C) Finance Minister

(D) Prime Minister of India

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The new ₹50 denomination banknotes in the Mahatma Gandhi (New) Series bear signature of the Governor, Reserve Bank of India. The note has motif of ‘Hampi with Chariot’ on the reverse, depicting the country’s cultural heritage.

Concept note-2: -The notes bear the signature of Dr. Bimal Jalan, Governor of the Reserve Bank.

Concept note-3: -As a result, the one rupee note is the only note bearing the signature of the Finance Secretary and not the Governor of the RBI.

Concept note-4: -The One Rupee note is issued by the Ministry of Finance and it bears the signatures of Finance Secretary, while other notes bear the signature of Governor RBI. Q.
