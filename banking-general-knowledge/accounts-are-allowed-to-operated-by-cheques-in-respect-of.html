Accounts are allowed to operated by Cheques in respect of

(A) Both Savings Accounts and Fixed Deposit accounts

(B) ** Savings account and Current Accounts

(C) Both Savings and Loan Accounts

(D) Both savings bank accounts and cash accounts only

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Both savings bank accounts and cash accounts only.

Concept note-2: -What is current account? Essentially, a current account is a deposit account that allows you to make payments in the form of cheques which is a major factor that helps distinguish between savings bank account and current account.

Concept note-3: -Banks donâ€™t issue debit cards for savings accounts; they rarely allow you to write checks for payments and purchases from savings.

Concept note-4: -Standard current accounts generally have no fees and come with a cheque book and/or a debit card. A standard current account may also offer an overdraft facility, which you will generally have to pay interest on.

Concept note-5: -A savings account is most suitable for people who are salaried employees or have a monthly income, whereas, Current Accounts work best for traders and entrepreneurs who need to access their accounts frequently. Savings accounts earn interest at a rate of around 4%, while there is no such earning from a Current Account.
