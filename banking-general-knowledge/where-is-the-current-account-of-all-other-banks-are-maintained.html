Where is the current account of all other banks are maintained?

(A) Finance Ministry

(B) ** RBI

(C) State Bank of India

(D) World Bank

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Bank also maintains Current accounts in the “Other Deposit Ledger’ of local bodies, Quasi-Government Institutions, Foreign Central Banks, International Organisations, Indian and Foreign Financial Institutions, Mutual Funds, Primary Dealers, Satellite Dealers etc.

Concept note-2: -1 Borrowers can open current accounts with any one of the banks with which it has CC/OD facility, provided that the bank has at least 10 per cent of the aggregate exposure of the banking system to that borrower.

Concept note-3: -It is the responsibility of each bank maintaining current account with the Reserve Bank to ensure that sufficient balance is available in the account to avoid defaults in payments and settlements.

Concept note-4: -The Banking Regulation Act, 1949 empowers the Reserve Bank of India to inspect and supervise commercial banks. These powers are exercised through on-site inspection and off site surveillance.

Concept note-5: -Further, as per Section 21 of the said Act, RBI has the right to transact Government business of the Union in India. State Government transactions are carried out by RBI in terms of the agreement entered into with the State Governments in terms of section 21 A of the Act.
