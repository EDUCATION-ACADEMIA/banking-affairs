The foreign shareholding in the small finance bank would be as per the FDI policy for____as amended from time to time.

(A) ** Private Sector Banks

(B) Public Sector Banks

(C) Foreign Sector Banks

(D) Regional Rural Banks

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -As per the current FDI policy, the aggregate foreign investment in a private sector bank from all sources will be allowed upto a maximum of 74 per cent of the paid-up capital of the bank (automatic upto 49 per cent and approval route beyond 49 per cent to 74 per cent).

Concept note-2: -The aggregate limit for all FIIs/FPIs/Qualified Foreign Investors (QFIs) cannot exceed 24% of the total paid-up capital. This can be raised to 49% of the total paid-up capital by the bank concerned through a resolution by its Board of Directors followed by a special resolution to that effect by its General Body.

Concept note-3: -Up to 100% FDI permitted under Government route: Banking and Public Sector – 20%
