Which was the first mutual fund started in India?

(A) SBI Mutual Fund

(B) Indian Bank Mutual Fund

(C) Kotak Pioneer Mutual Fund

(D) ** Unit Trust of India

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Unit Scheme 1964 (US ‘64) was the first scheme launched by UTI. At the end of 1988, UTI had ₹ 6, 700 crores of Assets Under Management (AUM).

Concept note-2: -First Phase-1964-1987 Unit Trust of India (UTI) was established in 1963 by an Act of Parliament.

Concept note-3: -The modern mutual fund that we know today first appeared in Boston in 1924 with the introduction of the Massachusetts Investors’ Trust, which was the first mutual fund with an open-end capitalization, allowing the fund to continuously issue and redeem its shares.

Concept note-4: -The second is the UTI Mutual fund carved out of Unit Trust of India, which functions under SEBI MF regulations from February 1, 2003.
