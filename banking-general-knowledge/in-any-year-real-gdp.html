In any year, real GDP

(A) ** might be greater or less than potential GDP

(B) will always be greater than potential GDP because of the tendency of nations to incur inflamation

(C) always equals potential GDP

(D) must always be less than potential GDP

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In any given year, an economy might have a real GDP that is greater or less than the potential GDP. When the economy is experiencing a recessionary gap, real GDP is usually less than the potential GDP. On the other hand, an expansionary gap results to a real GDP that is greater than the potential GDP.

Concept note-2: -real GDP fluctuates about potential GDP. The real GDP (gross domestic product) for a country fluctuates about the potential GDP, which is the GDP corresponding to full employment level. Thus, the real GDP could be equal to potential GDP, less than the potential GDP or more than the potential GDP.

Concept note-3: -Understanding an Inflationary Gap The real GDP can exceed the potential GDP, resulting in an inflationary gap. The inflationary gap denotes the relative rise in real GDP that causes an economy to increase its consumption, leading prices to climb in the long run.

Concept note-4: -In inflationary periods, real GDP will be lower than nominal GDP. In deflationary times, real GDP will be higher.
