Which of the following bank has recently signed an MoU with Kathmandu-based National Banking Institute (NBI) for the development of human resources in Nepal.

(A) EXIM Bank

(B) Bank of Baroda

(C) Punjab National Bank

(D) ** State Bank of India

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Detailed Solution. The correct answer is State Bank of India.

Concept note-2: -Nepal SBI Bank Ltd (NSBL) established in July 1993 as a subsidiary of State Bank of India (SBI). SBI holds 55% of ownership, Nepal Employee Provident Fund (Karmachari Sanchaya Kosh) holds 15% and the balance 30% shares are held with the general public.

Concept note-3: -About Nepal SBI Bank Ltd-Nepal (NSBL) is a subsidiary of State Bank of India (SBI) having 55 percent of ownership. The local partner viz. Employee Provident Fund holds 15% equity and General Public 30%.

Concept note-4: -Nepal SBI: Pure banking, nothing else.

Concept note-5: -NSBL commenced operation with effect from 7 July 1993 with one full-fledged office at Durbar Marg, Kathmandu with 18 staff members. The staff strength has since increased to 910 as on Ashadh on NSBL working in 91 branches, 21 extension counters, 7 Provincial Offices, 9 branchless banking & a Corporate Office.
