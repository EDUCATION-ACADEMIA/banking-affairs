Which of the following is not considered as negotiable instrument underthe Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881?

(A) Bill of Exchange

(B) Promissory Note

(C) Share Certificate

(D) ** Cheque Payable to Bearer

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A crossed cheque can only be paid in account & hence it is not a negotiable instrument.

Concept note-2: -A cheque is a Negotiable Instrument, which can be further negotiated by means of endorsement and is payable on demand. A cheque payable to bearer is negotiable by the delivery thereof, and when it is payable to order is negotiable by the holder by endorsement and delivery thereof.

Concept note-3: -(1) A “negotiable instrument” means a promissory note, bill of exchange or cheque payable either to order or to bearer.

Concept note-4: -FDR is not a negotiable instrument.
