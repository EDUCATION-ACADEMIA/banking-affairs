In India, the Real Time Gross Settlement has been implemented by____

(A) SEBI

(B) IRDA

(C) ** RBI

(D) Ministry of Finance

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Reserve Bank owns and operates following CPS: i. Real Time Gross Settlement (RTGS) System – It is the country’s Large Value Payment System and was introduced in March 2004.

Concept note-2: -It may be recalled that the Reserve Bank of India’s Real Time Gross Settlement (RTGS) System went live on March 26, 2004. Initially, it was open to the settlement of inter-bank transactions. From April 29, 2004, the RTGS System has been open also for the settlement of customer transactions.

Concept note-3: -What does RTGS stand for? Ans. The acronym ‘RTGS’ stands for Real Time Gross Settlement, which can be explained as a system where there is continuous and real-time settlement of fund-transfers, individually on a transaction-by-transaction basis (without netting).

Concept note-4: -Transactions through National Electronic Funds Transfer (NEFT) and Real Time Gross Settlement (RTGS) System will continue upto 2400 hours as hitherto on March 31, 2022. 4.
