Bad debts recovered is a____for the organisation.

(A) Expense

(B) Debt

(C) ** Income

(D) Asset

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Bad debts must be reported to the IRS as a loss. Bad debt recovery must be claimed as part of its gross income.

Concept note-2: -Irrecoverable debts are also referred to as ‘bad debts’ and an adjustment to two figures is needed. The amount goes into the statement of profit or loss as an expense and is deducted from the receivables figure in the statement of financial position.

Concept note-3: -Technically, “bad debt” is classified as an expense. It is reported along with other selling, general, and administrative costs. In either case, bad debt represents a reduction in net income, so in many ways, bad debt has characteristics of both an expense and a loss account.

Concept note-4: -Bad Debts Recovered If the amount recovered doesn’t exceed the expected, then the remaining amount will be treated as bad debts. If the amount received exceeds the recoverable amount, then the excess amount received will be treated as the income in the financial year of the receipt.
