The effect of a crossing a cheque is

(A) ** The payee can obtain payment only through a bank account

(B) The payee Is compelled to open an account

(C) The payee will have to endorse the cheque to a bank

(D) None of these

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A crossed check is any check that is crossed with two parallel lines, either across the whole check or through the top left-hand corner of the check. This double-line notation signifies that the check may only be deposited directly into a bank account.

Concept note-2: -Adding a crossing to a cheque increases its security in that it cannot be cashed at a bank counter but must be paid into an account in exactly the same name as the payee or indorsee indicated on the check.

Concept note-3: -General Cheque Crossing For the aim of general crossing 2 crosswise parallel lines at the corner of the cheque are necessary. Thus, during this case, the holder of the cheque or the receiver can receive the payment solely through a checking account and not over the counter.
