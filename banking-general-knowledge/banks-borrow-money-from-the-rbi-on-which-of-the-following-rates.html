Banks borrow money from the RBI on which of the following rates?

(A) Reverse Repo Rate

(B) ** Repo Rate

(C) SLR

(D) CRR

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Repo rate is the rate by which the central bank gives loans to the commercial banks. Thus, banks borrow money from Reserve Bank Of India by Repo rate. Repo rate is also used as a monetary method to control inflation.

Concept note-2: -Repo Rate (RR) is the rate at which the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) lends money to commercial banks or financial institutions in India against government securities. The current Repo Rate 2022 is at 4.40%.

Concept note-3: -Repo rate is the rate at which commercial banks borrow money from the central bank of a country (which in the case of India is the Reserve Bank of India or RBI) when they are in the need of funds.
