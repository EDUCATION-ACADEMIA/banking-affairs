Which one of the following is the major component of the money supply in the Indian Economy?

(A) Currency component

(B) Deposit component

(C) Treasury bill with public

(D) ** Both A and B

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Both the currency in circulation and the deposits with banks are key components of the money supply.

Concept note-2: -The main source of money supply in India is in the form of bank deposits and cash. RBI monitors the money supply in the economy and has the power to print and issue currency. Base money is the money issued by the Central Bank.

Concept note-3: -Coins, paper currency and deposits are the components of money supply in India.

Concept note-4: -The three different components of M1 are Currency and coins with public, Demand deposits of Commercial Banks, and Other deposits with RBI. All of these components can be easily used as a medium of exchange; therefore, it is the most liquid measure of the money supply.
