Commercial bank accept deposits from the public in the form of:

(A) Chequeable deposits

(B) Non-chequeable deposits

(C) ** Both (a) and (b)

(D) Gold deposits

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Banks accept deposits as chequeable deposits and non-chequeable deposits.

Concept note-2: -Accepts deposit : The bank takes deposits in the form of saving, current, and fixed deposits. The surplus balances collected from the firm and individuals are lent to the temporary requirements of the commercial transactions.

Concept note-3: -The major types of deposits are checking accounts, savings accounts, and time deposits. Access to finance can expand opportunities for all with higher levels of access and use of banking services associated with lower financing obstacles for people and businesses.

Concept note-4: -As per the commercial bank definition, it is a financial institution whose purpose is to accept deposits from people and provide loans and other facilities. Commercial banks provide basic services of banking to their customers and small to medium-sized businesses.

Concept note-5: -Commercial bank is the bank of the public.
