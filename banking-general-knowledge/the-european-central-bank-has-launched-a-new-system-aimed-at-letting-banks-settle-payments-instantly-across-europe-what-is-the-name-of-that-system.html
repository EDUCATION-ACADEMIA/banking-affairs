The European Central Bank has launched a new system aimed at letting banks settle payments instantly across Europe. What is the name of that system?

(A) SWIFT

(B) ** TIPS

(C) NEFT

(D) MPIJ

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -To speed up the development of instant payments in euro, the ERPB invited the European Payments Council (EPC) to develop a pan-European instant payment scheme. The scheme is based on the EPCâ€™s existing SEPA credit transfer (SCT) scheme and is called SEPA Instant Credit Transfer (SCT Inst).

Concept note-2: -TIPS was developed as an extension of TARGET2 and settles payments in central bank money. TIPS currently only settles payment transfers in euro. However, the central banks of Sweden, Denmark and Norway have shown interest in joining TIPS and settle instant payments in their national currencies.

Concept note-3: -TIPS offers final and irrevocable instant payments settlement in euro on a 24/7/365 basis. Participating payment service providers can open dedicated cash accounts (TIPS DCAs) in the books of their respective central bank. The NBB will assist Belgian TIPS participants in opening and maintaining their TIPS DCA(s).

Concept note-4: -The SEPA harmonizes the way cashless payments are transacted between euro countries. European consumers, businesses, and government agents who make payments by direct debit, instant card transfer, and credit transfers use the SEPA architecture.
