‘Sensitive sector’ as defined by RBI include(s)

(A) capital market

(B) real estate

(C) commodities

(D) ** All of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The RBI categorizes three sectors as Sensitive sectors – ‘Capital Market”, “Real Estate” & “Commodities”’.

Concept note-2: -The sensitive category regroups industrials, energy, technology and communication services. The sector is somewhat correlated with the economy and business cycles, but not as strongly as the cyclical super sector. It is sort of in between the cyclical and the defensive.

Concept note-3: -The Reserve Bank regulates and supervises the major part of the financial system. The supervisory role of the Reserve Bank covers commercial banks, Urban Cooperative Banks (UCBs), some FIs and NBFCs.

Concept note-4: -RBI Act, 1934, has how many sections? Ans: RBI Act, 1934 comprises 61 sections and four schedules.
