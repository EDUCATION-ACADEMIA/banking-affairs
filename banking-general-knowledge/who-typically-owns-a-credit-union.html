Who typically owns a credit union?

(A) Federal Reserve Bank

(B) Shareholders

(C) ** Members

(D) The U.S. government

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Credit unions are owned and governed by its members. Any person who becomes a member can actively participate in the affairs of the organization by direct voting.

Concept note-2: -YOU ARE PART OWNER. Credit unions are owned and controlled by the people, or members, who use their services. Your vote counts. A volunteer board of directors is elected by members to manage a credit union.

Concept note-3: -Their Customers Aren’t Just Members-They’re Stakeholders “On the other hand, credit unions are owned by the members, so in a credit union, you’re not just a member but also a stakeholder. If you have $20 in your account, you have the same voting rights as someone with $20, 000 in their account.

Concept note-4: -Being a credit union member means you share your financial institution’s ownership, vision and profits. It gives you the opportunity to shape your personal banking experience, as well as the impact your banking has on your local community. Membership has meaning and value.

Concept note-5: -A credit union is a cooperative, not-for-profit financial institution organized to promote thrift and provide credit to members. It is member-owned and controlled through a board of directors elected by the membership.
