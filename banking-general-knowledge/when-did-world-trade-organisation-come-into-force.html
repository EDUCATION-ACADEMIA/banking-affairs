When did World Trade Organisation come into force?

(A) 2nd January, 1948

(B) ** 1st January, 1995

(C) 12th January, 1948

(D) 15th January, 1946

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The WTO began life on 1 January 1995, but its trading system is half a century older. Since 1948, the General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade (GATT) had provided the rules for the system.

Concept note-2: -Birth of the WTO The WTOâ€™s creation on 1 January 1995 marked the biggest reform of international trade since the end of the Second World War. Whereas the GATT mainly dealt with trade in goods, the WTO and its agreements also cover trade in services and intellectual property.

Concept note-3: -On 1 January 1995, the WTO replaced GATT, which had been in existence since 1947, as the organization overseeing the multilateral trading system.

Concept note-4: -Trade negotiations. The World Trade Organization came into being in 1995. One of the youngest of the international organizations, the WTO is the successor to the General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade (GATT) established in the wake of the Second World War.
