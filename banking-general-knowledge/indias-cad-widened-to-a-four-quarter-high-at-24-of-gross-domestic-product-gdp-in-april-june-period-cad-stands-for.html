India’s CAD widened to a four-quarter-high at 2.4% of gross domestic product (GDP) in April-June period. CAD stands for

(A) Current Account Decrease

(B) Current Amount Deficit

(C) ** Current Account Deficit

(D) Common Account Deficit

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The current account deficit for Q1:2022-23 has been revised downwards from US$ 23.9 billion (2.8 per cent of GDP) due to downward adjustment in Customs data.

Concept note-2: -The country’s current account deficit (CAD) widened to 4.4 per cent of gross domestic product (GDP) in the second quarter of the fiscal 2022-23 on higher merchandise trade deficit.

Concept note-3: -As a percentage of GDP, India’s July-September CAD is 4.4 percent compared to 2.2 percent in April-June and 1.3 percent in July-September 2021. India’s current account deficit (CAD) surged to an all-time high of $36.4 billion in July-September, data released on December 29 by the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) showed.

Concept note-4: -India’s current account deficit (CAD) decreased to US$ 13.4 billion (1.5 per cent of GDP) in Q4:2021-22 from US$ 22.2 billion (2.6 per cent of GDP) in Q3:2021-22.
