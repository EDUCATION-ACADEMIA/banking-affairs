Applications of funds for Islamic Bank EXCEPT

(A) Reserve requirement

(B) Financing

(C) ** Customer Deposits

(D) Investment

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The sources of funds of an Islamic bank include deposits in various accounts and deposits in special investment accounts that are earmarked for bor-rowing by corporate investors to fund specific projects. Shareholder funds are also a source of funds for Islamic banks.

Concept note-2: -There are basically two principal sources of funds, namely work capital of shareholders and customer deposits. A separate accounting system is adopted for a dual window banking operation, which separates all Islamic bank funds from conventional banking and to avoid the arrival of funds.

Concept note-3: -Joint or general investment deposits are funds received by Islamic banks from customers who wish to participate in the banksâ€™ financing or investing activities in an organized and continuous manner, without any conditions being imposed on the banks.
