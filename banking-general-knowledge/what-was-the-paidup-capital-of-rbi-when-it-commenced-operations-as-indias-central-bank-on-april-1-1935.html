What was the paidup capital of RBI when it commenced operations as India’s central bank on April 1 1935?

(A) Rs 1 crore

(B) ** Rs 5 crore

(C) Rs 10 crore

(D) Rs 100 crore

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -1935: The Reserve Bank commenced operations as India’s central bank on April 1 as a private shareholders’ bank with a paid up capital of rupees five crores (rupees fifty million).

Concept note-2: -RBI was formed. At the time of establishment, the authorized capital of the Reserve Bank of India was ₹50 million. The government’s share in this was only ₹2-2.2 million.

Concept note-3: -5 Crore, divided into shares of Rs. 100 each fully paid up. In the beginning, this entire capital was owned by private shareholders.

Concept note-4: -The Reserve Bank of India Act, 1934 (II of 1934) provides the statutory basis of the functioning of the Bank, which commenced operations on April 1, 1935. * To operate the credit and currency system of the country to its advantage.

Concept note-5: -Under the Reserve Bank of India, Act, 1934 (RBI Act, 1934) (as amended in 2016), RBI is entrusted with the responsibility of conducting monetary policy in India with the primary objective of maintaining price stability while keeping in mind the objective of growth.
