The financial year for banks is April-March, but what is the financial year for RBI?

(A) January-December

(B) April-March

(C) ** July-June

(D) October-September

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The accounting year for the Reserve Bank of India was changed to April-March (earlier July-June) from the financial year 2020-21 onwards. Where available, this chapter has been updated beyond March 2022.

Concept note-2: -Thus, data presented in the Chapter for the current year, i.e., 2021-22 and earlier years (July to June) are for twelve months as compared to the nine months period for the previous year (July 2020 to March 2021).

Concept note-3: -Detailed Solution. The current RBI’s accounting year runs from July to June while the government follows the April-March fiscal year.

Concept note-4: -“…the primary objective of monetary policy is to maintain price stability while keeping in mind the objective of growth.” The meeting schedule of the Monetary Policy Committee (MPC) for 2022-2023 was announced in the Reserve Bank’s Press Release: 2021-2022/1931 dated March 30, 2022.
