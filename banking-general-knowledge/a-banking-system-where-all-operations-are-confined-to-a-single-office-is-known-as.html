A banking system where all operations are confined to a single office is known as____

(A) ** Unit Banking

(B) Group Banking

(C) Chain Banking

(D) Branch Banking

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Unit Banking is a system of banking wherein a bank operates in a limited area, does not open any branches in other places and is more responsive to local needs. These independent and isolated units have to take care of the entire banking operations and maintain good health.

Concept note-2: -Unit banking refers to a single, usually very small bank that provides financial services to its local community. Typically, a unit bank is independent and operates without any connecting banks or branches in the area.

Concept note-3: -Unit Banking In the unit banking system, the banking operations are carried through a single office and confined to a particular area. The banks maintain no branches.

Concept note-4: -Unit banking, in general, means a bank that does not have any branches. It is typically small in size and provides services to only a specific area in which it operates. No main branch controls its functioning. On the other hand, branch banking is fully controlled by the main branch.
