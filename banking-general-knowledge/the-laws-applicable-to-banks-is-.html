The laws applicable to banks is ____

(A) Sale of goods Act, 1930

(B) Consumer Protection Act, 2019

(C) Indian Contract Act, 1872

(D) ** Companies Act 2013

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Section 1(4)(c) of the Companies Act, 2013 expressly states that the Act is applicable to banking companies as well, to the extent that it is consistent with the Banking Regulation Act, 1949 .

Concept note-2: -Companies Act 2013 is applicable to other sectors such as Banking companies, insurance companies, electricity companies etc. However, the act excludes these companies from its purview for some sections which are governed under the special acts under which these companies are formed.

Concept note-3: -The Act provides a framework under which commercial banking in India is supervised and regulated. The Act supplements the Companies Act, 1956. Primary Agricultural Credit Society and cooperative land mortgage banks are excluded from the Act.
