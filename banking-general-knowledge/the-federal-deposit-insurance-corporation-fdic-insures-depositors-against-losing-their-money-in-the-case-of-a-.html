The Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation (FDIC) insures depositors against losing their money in the case of a ____

(A) lost debit card

(B) stolen paycheck

(C) ** bank failure

(D) death in the family

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The FDIC protects the money depositors place in insured banks in the unlikely event of an insured-bank failure. Each depositor is insured to at least $250, 000 per insured bank. FDIC deposit insurance covers all types of deposits held at an insured bank.

Concept note-2: -Since the founding of the Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation in 1933 no depositor has lost a penny of FDIC-insured funds.

Concept note-3: -The standard insurance amount is $250, 000 per depositor, per insured bank, for each account ownership category.
