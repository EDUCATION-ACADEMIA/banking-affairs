Which Bank has become the first domestic bank to link the interest rate it offers on savings account deposits to an external benchmark-the Reserve Bank of India’s repo rate?

(A) Indian Bank

(B) Punjab National Bank

(C) Bank of Baroda

(D) ** State Bank of India

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Will Other Banks Follow? While SBI has been the first to link deposit and lending rates to an external benchmark, other large banks may follow suit.

Concept note-2: -Fixed deposit provides highest rate of interest as a large amount of money is deposited in this account for a longer duration.

Concept note-3: -SBI home loan interest rate June 2022 India’s largest lender State Bank of India (SBI) has increased its repo-rate linked lending rate to 7.55% from 7.05%, effective from June 15, 2022.

Concept note-4: -The interest rate on savings bank deposit has been progressively reduced by the Reserve Bank.

Concept note-5: -Earn 7.3%* Interest on FD Rates are subject to change as per the bank’s discretion. Bank of India offers FD interest rates of 3.00%-7.05% p.a. to regular citizens and 3.00%-7.55% p.a. to senior citizens on tenures ranging from 7 days to 10 years. The interest rate on Bank of India Tax Saving FDs is 6.00% p.a.
