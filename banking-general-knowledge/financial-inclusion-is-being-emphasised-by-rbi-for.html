Financial inclusion is being emphasised by RBI for

(A) ** providing basic banking services to the unbanked areas

(B) providing loans to the unbanked population

(C) opening accounts for the poor

(D) bringing branches of all banks under one authority

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -This role encapsulates the essence of renewed national focus on Financial Inclusion, promoting financial education and literacy and making credit available to productive sectors of the economy including the rural and MSME sector.

Concept note-2: -Financial inclusion means that individuals and businesses have access to useful and affordable financial products and services that meet their needs – transactions, payments, savings, credit and insurance – delivered in a responsible and sustainable way.

Concept note-3: -It is an ambitious strategy which aims to strengthen the ecosystem for various modes of digital financial services in all Tier-II to Tier VI centres to create the necessary infrastructure to move towards a less-cash society by March 2022.
