The rate at which Banks borrow money from the Reserve Bank is called____

(A) ** Repo rate

(B) Reverse repo rate

(C) Bank rate

(D) Base rate

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Definition: Repo rate is the rate at which the central bank of a country (Reserve Bank of India in case of India) lends money to commercial banks in the event of any shortfall of funds. Repo rate is used by monetary authorities to control inflation.

Concept note-2: -Repo Rate (RR) is the rate at which the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) lends money to commercial banks or financial institutions in India against government securities. The current Repo Rate 2022 is at 4.40%.

Concept note-3: -The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) hiked the repo on 7 December 2022 by 35 basis points to 6.25%. Earlier, the Repo Rate was 5.90% and this is the fifth-rate hike in this financial year by the central bank. The RBI increased the repo rate by 40 bps in May and 50 bps in June, August, and September.

Concept note-4: -The current repo rate is 6.25% as of 7th December 2022, while the reverse repo rate remains unchanged is 3.35%.
